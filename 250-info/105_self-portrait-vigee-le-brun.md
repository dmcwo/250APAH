## #105 — Self-Portrait

**Elisabeth Louise Vigée Le Brun** · 1790 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Uffizi, Florence · 100 × 81 cm

### Summary

A self-confident **self-portrait** by the most successful female painter of 18th-century France — a testimony to her professional status.

- **Historical Context.** Vigée Le Brun was **court painter to Marie Antoinette** and one of very few women admitted to the **French Royal Academy** (1783). She fled Paris at the outbreak of the **French Revolution** (1789) and lived in exile across Europe; this painting was commissioned by the **Uffizi Gallery** in Florence for its famous collection of artists' self-portraits, a high honor.
- **Function.** Self-advertisement asserting her skill on equal terms with male peers, and cementing her international reputation while in exile.
- **Content.** Vigée Le Brun turns from her easel toward the viewer, palette and brushes in hand, in the act of painting a portrait of Marie Antoinette on her canvas. She wears a white muslin dress and a turban-like scarf — fashionable, intellectual, and faintly exotic.
- **Form.** Soft **Rococo-to-Neoclassical transitional** style: warm creamy flesh tones, loose brushwork in the dress, crisp detail in the tools of her trade. The gaze is direct, unforced, confident — claiming her place as painter-subject.

**Vocabulary:** self-portrait · French Royal Academy · Rococo
