## #52 — Hagia Sophia

**Anthemius of Tralles and Isidorus of Miletus, Byzantine** · 532–537 C.E. · Brick and ceramic elements with stone and mosaic veneer · Istanbul (Constantinople), Turkey · 269 × 240 × 180 ft

### Summary

**Justinian's** "Holy Wisdom" — a colossal Byzantine church whose **pendentive-supported central dome** seemed, to its 6th-century beholders, "suspended from heaven." Later a mosque, a museum, and again a mosque.

- **Historical Context.** The current building is the **third** Hagia Sophia on the site. The first two, begun under Constantine I and Constans I, were burned (the second during the **Nika riots** of 532). **Justinian** rebuilt immediately, commissioning the mathematicians **Anthemius of Tralles and Isidorus of Miletus**. Served as the Cathedral of Constantinople for over a millennium; after **Mehmed II**'s conquest in 1453 it became a **mosque**; secularized as a museum in 1934.
- **Function.** Great Byzantine **cathedral**, then **imperial mosque**, then museum. In all incarnations a theater for sacred light and imperial ceremony.
- **Content.** Originally a gold-and-mosaic interior with a giant cross at the dome's crown; iconic mosaics of Christ, the Virgin, and emperors (some recovered after the Iconoclasm that ended in 843). After 1453, Christian images were whitewashed, **minarets, a mihrab, a minbar, and medallions of Arabic calligraphy** were added.
- **Form.** A fusion of **longitudinal basilica and centralized building**. The **32-m main dome** rides on **pendentives**, anchored by two half-domes, supported by massive piers; windows at its base produce an effect of levitation. Derivative **Ionic** capitals cut with lace-like acanthus; four slim pencil **minarets** added under Ottoman rule.

**Vocabulary:** cathedral · apse · basilica · squinch · pendentive · dome · cornice · nave · capital · spandrel · entablature · minaret · Mecca · mihrab · calligraphy
