## #77 — Isenheim altarpiece

**Matthias Grünewald** · c. 1512–1516 C.E. · Oil on wood · Unterlinden Museum, Colmar (originally Isenheim, Germany) · 9′ 9½″ × 10′ 9″

### Summary

A **Northern Renaissance** altarpiece whose brutal Crucifixion confronts the suffering of the hospital's dying patients.

- **Historical Context.** Painted by Grünewald for the monastic hospital at **Isenheim** run by the **Antonite order**, which treated victims of **ergotism** ("St. Anthony's Fire") — a fungal poisoning from rye causing gangrene, hallucinations, and death. Northern Renaissance style coincides with the dawn of the **Protestant Reformation**.
- **Function.** Hospital altarpiece for worship and for comforting the sick. Images of suffering saints and Christ's wounds offered a mirror to — and meaning for — the patients' own afflictions. Altarpieces also framed the **Eucharist**.
- **Content.** A folding altarpiece with **three positions**. First (closed): the Crucifixion with John the Baptist, Mary, John the Evangelist, Mary Magdalene, flanked by Sts. Sebastian and Anthony; predella shows the Lamentation. Second: Annunciation, Angel Concert with Madonna and Child, Resurrection. Third: St. Paul and St. Anthony in the Desert; Temptation of St. Anthony; wooden sculptures of Sts. Jerome, Anthony, and Augustine.
- **Form.** Complex structure with **two sets of movable wings** over a sculpted shrine. Christ's body is distorted, pocked, and ulcerated — visually matched to ergotism lesions. Unsettling bright/black color contrasts; hybrid demons in the Temptation; bodies not idealized.

**Vocabulary:** altarpiece · movable wings · panel · predella · ergotism · St. Anthony · Northern Renaissance
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.3, 1.4, 3.2
