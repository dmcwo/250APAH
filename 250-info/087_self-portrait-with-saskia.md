## #87 — Self-Portrait with Saskia

**Rembrandt van Rijn** · 1636 C.E. · Etching · Fitzwilliam Museum, Cambridge · 10.5 × 9.5 cm (plate)

### Summary

An intimate **Dutch Baroque etching** — a marriage portrait doubling as a self-advertisement for the artist.

- **Historical Context.** Rembrandt (b. Leiden, 1606) was the leading Dutch Baroque painter and a pioneer who elevated **etching** from reproductive technique to independent art form. Made two years after his marriage to **Saskia van Uylenburgh**, the wealthy niece of his dealer. The Dutch Golden Age valued secular genre, portraiture, and printmaking for a middle-class market.
- **Function.** A personal **marriage commemoration** and a display piece for prospective patrons, advertising Rembrandt's virtuosity in etching. Self-portraits let him experiment with costumes and lighting.
- **Content.** Rembrandt at his drawing table in fanciful historical dress (plumed beret, cloak), **porte-crayon** in hand, meets the viewer's eye directly; young Saskia sits behind him, looking off into middle distance. The setup suggests he is drawing her, and the viewer has interrupted a quiet studio moment.
- **Form.** Loose, sketch-like **crosshatching** and expressive line. Deep bite creates rich dark shadows that pull Rembrandt forward; Saskia is rendered in lighter lines receding back. High-contrast Baroque chiaroscuro in black ink alone.

**Vocabulary:** etching · printmaking · crosshatching · bite (acid)
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 2.1, 3.5
