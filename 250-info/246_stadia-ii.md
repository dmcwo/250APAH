## #246 — Stadia II

**Julie Mehretu (Ethiopian-American)** · 2004 C.E. · Ink and acrylic on canvas · Carnegie Museum of Art, Pittsburgh · 108 × 144 in

### Summary

A monumental **abstract painting** overlaying architectural plans of global stadiums with floating flags, logos, and confetti — one panel of a triptych on nationalism, sport, and mass spectacle.

- **Historical Context.** **Julie Mehretu** (b. 1970 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia; raised in Michigan) earned an MFA at RISD in 1997. Her mature work builds dense palimpsests of maps, architectural drawings, and abstract marks that read as atmospheric, tectonic, and political at once. *Stadia II* is part of a 2004 triptych commissioned while Mehretu was artist-in-residence at Houston's Glassell School; it drew on the Russian Constructivist tradition and on stadium architecture as a site of nationalism, corporate sponsorship, and propaganda.
- **Function.** Large-scale contemporary painting for museum display. Invites viewers to lose themselves in cascading lines and shards of color while meditating on the **choreography of crowds** at sports events, political rallies, and acts of terror.
- **Content.** Curved architectural shells of **stadium interiors** drawn in ink at the bottom; a centrifugal eruption of color — small circles and dots (confetti), triangles and blocks (flag fragments, corporate logos, religious symbols) — bursts outward from the center. Gray painterly marks rise like smoke.
- **Form.** Layered technique: projected architectural plans traced in ink, sealed under acrylic-and-silica ground, then further drawing and paint layered above. Vast scale. Limited color palette of red, black, white, and blue dominates.

**Vocabulary:** centrifugal · stratified · tectonic geology · constructivism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.2, 1.3
