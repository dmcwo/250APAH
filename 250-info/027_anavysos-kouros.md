## #27 — Anavysos Kouros

**Archaic Greek** · c. 530 B.C.E. · Marble with remnants of paint · National Archaeological Museum, Athens · 6 ft 4 in tall

### Summary

A life-size Archaic **kouros** — a grave marker for a youth named **Kroisos** who died in battle — whose heavier musculature and bolder posture mark the move away from Egyptian-style rigidity.

- **Historical Context.** Made in 6th-century B.C.E. Greece, an Archaic culture wealthy from Mediterranean trade. An inscription found near the statue reads "Stay and mourn at the monument of dead Kroisos, whom raging Ares slew as he fought in the front ranks," identifying the subject as an aristocratic youth killed in war.
- **Function.** A **funerary marker** atop the grave of Kroisos; simultaneously an **idealized image of male youth**, not a portrait.
- **Content.** Standing frontal male nude — left foot advanced, fists clenched, long braided hair, subtle **Archaic smile**. Slim waist, broad shoulders, and strongly defined thighs and calves.
- **Form.** Freestanding **marble** once painted (traces remain on the lips). The rigid, symmetrical pose and frontality derive from Egyptian precedent, but the **rounder musculature** and more lifelike proportions (~1:7 head-to-body) signal a move toward naturalism.

**Vocabulary:** kouros · Archaic smile · mortuary · idealism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.1, 2.2
