## #86 — Henri IV Receives the Portrait of Marie de' Medici, from the Marie de' Medici Cycle

**Peter Paul Rubens** · 1621–1625 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Louvre, Paris · 394 × 295 cm

### Summary

Rubens' **Flemish Baroque** allegory transforms a royal marriage negotiation into a Mount-Olympus pageant.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by the Queen Mother **Marie de' Medici** for her gallery at the Palais du Luxembourg, Paris, after she was exiled by her son Louis XIII and then reconciled. One of 24 monumental canvases narrating — and glorifying — her life. Rubens was the leading Flemish painter, trained on Titian and the Venetians; he ran a large Antwerp workshop and served as a diplomat.
- **Function.** **Dynastic propaganda.** The cycle legitimizes Marie's regency and recasts her messy political history as divinely ordained destiny.
- **Content.** Winged **Cupids of Marriage and Love** present a portrait of Marie to **Henry IV** of France, who gazes at it, smitten. **Jupiter** and **Juno**, gods of marriage, approve from clouds above; a personified **France** (helmeted) leans into Henri to counsel political union. Marie and a cherub make eye contact with the viewer.
- **Form.** Typical Rubens Baroque: writhing bodies in dramatic diagonals, warm Venetian palette with shimmering flesh, swirling drapery, and lavish sensuousness. Real event mixed freely with mythological allegory.

**Vocabulary:** Baroque · allegory · dynastic propaganda
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4, 3.2
