## #144 — Fountain (second version)

**Marcel Duchamp** (Dada) · 1950 C.E. (original 1917) · Readymade glazed sanitary china with black paint · Philadelphia Museum of Art · ~360 × 480 × 610 mm

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Marcel_Duchamp%2C_1917%2C_Fountain%2C_photograph_by_Alfred_Stieglitz.jpg/250px-Marcel_Duchamp%2C_1917%2C_Fountain%2C_photograph_by_Alfred_Stieglitz.jpg" width="240" alt="Fountain by Duchamp">

### Summary

The founding work of **readymade** art and an icon of **Dada**.

- **Historical Context.** **Dada** emerged in Europe during WWI as a reaction against the violence, nationalism, and rationalism of the war — artists of the **Lost Generation** rejecting the whole apparatus of high art. Duchamp, in New York, helped found the **Society of Independent Artists** whose 1917 salon was advertised as unjuried; he submitted *Fountain* under the false name "R. Mutt" to test that promise. The board rejected it for indecency. The original was lost; only Alfred Stieglitz's photo and 17 authorized 1960s replicas survive.
- **Function.** Not a urinal — a provocation. A challenge to the institution of art: can a mass-produced object become art by the artist's choice and signature alone?
- **Content.** A porcelain urinal from **J. L. Mott Iron Works**, rotated 90°, signed **"R. Mutt 1917"** — a pun on the Mutt and Jeff comic strip, the manufacturer, or German "Armut" (poverty).
- **Form.** A **readymade** — an ordinary industrial object recontextualized as sculpture. Its reorientation frustrates the object's function and forces the viewer to consider form, shadow, and shape instead. Duchamp had a long-running interest in representing a fourth spatial dimension.

**Vocabulary:** Dadaism · readymade
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3, 1.4
