## #157 — Templo Mayor (Main Temple)

**Mexica (Aztec)** · 1375–1520 C.E. · Stone (temple); volcanic stone (Coyolxauhqui Stone); jadeite (Olmec-style mask); basalt (Calendar Stone) · Tenochtitlan (modern Mexico City) · base 328 × 262 ft; h. ~90 ft

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Templo_Mayor_50.jpg/250px-Templo_Mayor_50.jpg" width="240" alt="Templo Mayor">

### Summary

The ritual heart of the **Aztec** capital — twin pyramids to **Tlaloc** and **Huitzilopochtli** at the navel of the universe.

- **Historical Context.** Construction began c. 1390 C.E. and continued through seven successive enlargements by Aztec rulers (notably **Motecuhzoma I**, 1454) until Cortés's forces razed the city in 1521. The design reflects the **Mexica cosmos**: four parts arranged around the **axis mundi**. Built on the spot where, according to the Mexica, Huitzilopochtli showed them the sign of the **eagle on the cactus eating a snake** — still on the Mexican flag.
- **Function.** Ritual and political center of the Aztec empire. The paired deities embodied **atl-tlachinolli** ("burnt water") — warfare, which produced the empire's wealth. Required **human sacrifice**, bloodletting, copal incense, and music.
- **Content.** A single stone platform carrying **twin pyramids**: left shrine (painted blue and white) to **Tlaloc**, god of rain, on a sacred mountain called Tonacatepetl; right shrine (painted red and white) to **Huitzilopochtli**, god of war and sun, on the Hill of Coatepec. Twin staircases lead up; serpent-head balustrades flank the bases. A **Wall of Skulls** (*tzompantli*) at the foot holds the plastered skulls of sacrificial victims.
- **Form.** Stone covered in **stucco**; broad pyramid steps and symmetrical twin temples. Integrated with the **Coyolxauhqui Stone** at the base of the Huitzilopochtli stair — the dismembered moon goddess — and with the **Calendar Stone** and Olmec-style jadeite mask in the surrounding precinct.

**Vocabulary:** axis mundi · Tlaloc · Huitzilopochtli · atl-tlachinolli · tzompantli
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.3, 1.4, 2.1, 2.2, 3.1
