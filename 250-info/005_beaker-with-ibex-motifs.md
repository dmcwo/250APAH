## #5 — Beaker with Ibex Motifs

**Susa I culture** · c. 4,200–3,500 B.C.E. · Painted terra cotta · Louvre, Paris (found at Susa, Iran) · 28.9 × 16.4 cm

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/79/Bushel_with_Ibex_Motifs.jpg" width="240" alt="Beaker with Ibex Motifs">

### Summary

A Susa I funerary beaker whose **geometrically abstracted animals** anticipate later Near Eastern ornament.

- **Historical Context.** Made by the earliest inhabitants of **Susa** (modern Shush, Iran), near the Zagros mountains — a site whose archaeology ties it to Uruk, the proto-Elamites, Sumerians, Akkadians, and later Achaemenids. The depicted animals (ibex, wading birds, saluki-like dogs) all lived in the surrounding mountains and grasslands.
- **Function.** Excavated from the **Susa acropolis burials**; one of many similar beakers thought to hold food for the deceased in the afterlife. The abstract symbol inside the ibex horns may be a **clan emblem** identifying the deceased.
- **Content.** Three stylized ibex with exaggerated sweeping horns encircling an abstract symbol; above them, a band of long-necked wading birds and another of elongated running dogs.
- **Form.** Wheel-thrown **terra cotta** (unglazed, from red clay). Animals built from modified geometric shapes — circles, triangles, rectangles — producing biomorphic silhouettes that dissolve into pattern at a distance. Simple two-color scheme: natural clay with dark brown paint. No background, no depth.

**Vocabulary:** abstraction · animal style · biomorphic · twisted perspective
