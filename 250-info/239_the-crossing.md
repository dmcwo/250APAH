## #239 — The Crossing

**Bill Viola (USA)** · 1996 C.E. · Video/sound installation (two channels of color video projection onto screens mounted back-to-back, amplified stereo sound) · Collection of the Bill Viola Studio and museums worldwide · Variable; projections roughly 16 ft × 23 ft

### Summary

A monumental video diptych in which a man is progressively consumed by **fire on one screen and engulfed by water on the other** — a contemporary elemental meditation on death, purification, and rebirth.

- **Historical Context.** **Bill Viola** (1951–2024) is a pioneering video artist whose work fuses new-media technology with deeply traditional subjects drawn from Christian mysticism, Sufi poetry, and Zen Buddhism. *The Crossing* premiered in 1996 during the maturation of single-channel video installation as a museum medium. Viola's slow time, devotional subjects, and theatrical scale made him the Old Master of video art.
- **Function.** A contemplative **sacramental object**. Visitors walk around the back-to-back screens and experience both destructions simultaneously; the accompanying thunderous soundtrack amplifies the body's response. Viola treats video as a vehicle for spiritual encounter.
- **Content.** A man slowly approaches the camera against a black ground. On one screen, a flame ignites at his feet, grows, and eventually engulfs him; on the other, a drip of water becomes a torrent that drowns him. Both reduce him to nothing; both last about ten minutes before looping.
- **Form.** Two high-resolution color video projections mounted back to back; each projected onto a large screen; amplified stereo sound. Extreme slow motion stretches moments of transformation.
