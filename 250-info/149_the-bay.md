## #149 — The Bay

**Helen Frankenthaler** (Color Field / Abstract Expressionism) · 1963 C.E. · Acrylic on canvas · Detroit Institute of Arts · 8′9″ × 6′9″

### Summary

A defining **Color Field** painting made with Frankenthaler's signature **soak-stain** technique.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in New York during the postwar era when abstract expressionists sought a universal visual language in response to WWII trauma. Frankenthaler developed the **soak-stain method** after a 1952 trip to Nova Scotia, thinning oil (later acrylic) paint so heavily that it soaked into **unprimed canvas** — generating translucent fields meant to evoke hills, rocks, and water. She was introduced to Pollock, Rothko, and David Smith through her partner, critic **Clement Greenberg**.
- **Function.** Non-narrative and expressive. Frankenthaler rarely named her sources, leaving each viewer's interpretation open; she argued that emotional impact mattered more than subject.
- **Content.** An amorphous blue **promontory** against a cream-and-moss-green border — read as a bay viewed from above. The blue shifts from near-indigo at top to lighter violets and blues below.
- **Form.** **Color Field** painting: simplicity of line, color as subject. The soak-stain technique embeds pigment into the canvas rather than sitting on top; by tilting the canvas Frankenthaler lets the paint find its own edges. 1963 is also when she shifted from oil to **acrylic**.

**Vocabulary:** Abstract Expressionism · Color Field · soak-stain method
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2
