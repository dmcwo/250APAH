## #236 — En la Barberia no se Llora (No Crying Allowed in the Barbershop)

**Pepón Osorio (Puerto Rican-American)** · 1994 C.E. · Mixed-media installation · Originally installed on Park Street, Hartford, Connecticut · Installation scale

### Summary

An immersive installation reconstructing a **Puerto Rican barbershop** pink-walled, overloaded with machismo kitsch, and screening videos of men weeping.

- **Historical Context.** **Pepón Osorio** (b. 1955 in Puerto Rico) trained as a social worker before becoming an installation artist. Commissioned by **Real Art Ways** and installed in a storefront in the heavily Puerto Rican community on Park Street in Hartford. Osorio deliberately placed the work within the community rather than in a museum, inviting Latino men (and their families) to confront expectations of **machismo** on their own turf. The Puerto Rican barbershop is a key site of male socialization.
- **Function.** **Community intervention**. Confronts machismo and homophobia by juxtaposing hypermasculine iconography with the taboo of men crying on camera. Most visitors were women — offering a moment of reflection on the men in their lives.
- **Content.** A barbershop lined with pink rose-patterned walls, plastic flowers, religious portraits, sports memorabilia, a Puerto Rican flag, toy action figures, baseballs, **chucherías** (trinkets), clear plastic car mats, and a red velour automobile-seat barber chair. **TV monitors** embedded in the mirrors loop videos of Latino men weeping, with their neighbors' reactions.
- **Form.** Osorio calls the style **"Nuyorican Baroque"** — excessive, saturated, ornamented. Every surface is loaded. The installation engages sight, sound, and enclosed space.

**Vocabulary:** Nuyorican Baroque · machismo · chucherías
