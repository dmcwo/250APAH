## #184 — Jowo Rinpoche, enshrined in the Jokhang Temple

**Tibetan** (Yarlung Dynasty) · early–mid 7th century C.E. · Gilt metals with semiprecious stones, pearls, paint, and votive offerings · Jokhang Temple, Lhasa, Tibet · ~5 ft

### Summary

The **most venerated Buddha image in Tibet** — a Shakyamuni Buddha said to have been modeled in the Buddha's own lifetime.

- **Historical Context.** According to tradition the celestial Indian architect **Vishwakarma** carved the Jowo Rinpoche during the life of the Buddha Shakyamuni (6th–5th century B.C.E.), so it preserves a true likeness. In the 7th century C.E., the Chinese princess **Wencheng Gongzhu** brought the sculpture to Tibet as part of her marriage dowry — the moment that aligns with the founding of **Vajrayana Buddhism** in Tibet. Renovated across 1,300 years; most recently after the **Cultural Revolution**; survived a 2018 fire at the Jokhang.
- **Function.** An **earthly proxy** for the Buddha after his *parinirvana*. Pilgrims visiting the Jokhang — Tibet's most sacred site — seek the Jowo's blessing as equivalent to being blessed by the Buddha himself.
- **Content.** Shakyamuni depicted at the moment of enlightenment, age 12. Seated in **padmasana** (lotus position) on a jeweled gold throne. Right hand in **bhumisparsha mudra** (calling earth to witness); left hand in **dhyana mudra** (meditation). A tall jeweled crown and blue hair top the gilt face; beneath ornate regalia he wears a simple monk's robe.
- **Form.** **Additive sculpture** — gilt metal core adorned with semiprecious stones, pearls, paint, and continual pilgrim offerings. Slightly over life-size.

**Vocabulary:** padmasana · rinpoche · dhyana mudra · bhumisparsha mudra
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4, 2.2
