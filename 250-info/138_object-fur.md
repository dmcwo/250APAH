## #138 — Object (*Le Déjeuner en fourrure*)

**Meret Oppenheim** (Surrealism) · 1936 C.E. · Fur-covered cup, saucer, and spoon (assemblage sculpture) · Museum of Modern Art, New York · cup Ø 10.9 cm; saucer Ø 23.7 cm; spoon L 20.2 cm

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5d/Meret_Oppenheim._Object._Paris%2C_1936_-MoMA.png/330px-Meret_Oppenheim._Object._Paris%2C_1936_-MoMA.png" width="240" alt="Object (Le Déjeuner en fourrure)">

### Summary

The single most-cited object of **Surrealist sculpture**.

- **Historical Context.** Made in 1936 in Paris when Oppenheim was 23, at the peak of **Surrealism**. The work was born in a café conversation with **Picasso** and **Dora Maar**: Oppenheim was wearing a fur-covered brass-tube bracelet; Picasso joked that anything could be covered in fur, and Oppenheim shot back, "Even this cup and saucer." Shown soon after at MoMA's 1936 *Fantastic Art, Dada, Surrealism* exhibition, where visitors voted it "the quintessential Surrealist object."
- **Function.** Non-utilitarian. A provocation — pure Surrealist dépaysement, making the familiar disturbing — and a symbol of domesticity and femininity in the café-culture of 1930s Paris.
- **Content.** A café teacup, saucer, and spoon, all completely wrapped in **Chinese gazelle fur** in shades of bistre and camel yellow.
- **Form.** An **assemblage** of found objects — a Surrealist technique of juxtaposition. Realistic proportions, smooth geometry (circle of cup, oval of saucer), but the entire surface replaced with fur texture. The contradiction between fur (luxury, sensuality) and the mouth-to-cup action drives the effect.

**Vocabulary:** Surrealism · automatism · assemblage sculpture
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3, 3.2

**Theme:** Animal Consumption

> There are two connections shown: 1) both pieces focus on how humans interact and use animals and 2) Corned beef shows an animal made out of unconventional material that humans use to contain foods and drinks while The Object shows a material that humans use to contain foods and drinks (the café cup) made out of parts of an animal.

#### → Pink Panther (Jeff Koons, 1988) — *not in image set*

**Theme:** Hollywood fantasy

> Both pieces address certain aspects of the bourgeoisie at their respective time periods. The pink panther is shown as worn and tired which may refer to, again, how humans treated animals at that time. The pink panther, however, has been criticized with anti-feminist sentiments while Meret Oppenheim was known for leading her way through a male dominated era and portraying potentially feminist ideas in her works of art.
