## #22 — Akhenaton, Nefertiti, and three daughters

**New Kingdom Egypt (Amarna), 18th Dynasty** · c. 1353–1335 B.C.E. · Limestone · Amarna, Egypt

### Summary

A small Amarna-period **sunken relief** of the royal family beneath the rayed disk of **Aten** — a famously intimate image of pharaonic life that documents Akhenaten's religious revolution.

- **Historical Context.** **Akhenaten** imposed a radical **monotheistic cult of Aten**, the sun disk, and moved Egypt's capital from Thebes to **Amarna**. He and **Nefertiti** presented themselves as the sole intermediaries between Aten and humanity. The experiment did not outlive him.
- **Function.** A **household shrine** for private worship in an Amarna home, reinforcing the royal family as the conduit to Aten — the rays of the sun disk terminate in hands offering **ankhs** to the king and queen.
- **Content.** Akhenaten kisses a daughter while Nefertiti holds two more; above them the **Aten** disk radiates hands bearing life. An informal, domestic royal scene unprecedented in pharaonic imagery.
- **Form.** **Sunken relief** in limestone. Bodies are **curvilinear and elongated** — swollen thighs, narrow shoulders, distinctive long faces — a radical break from the rigidly geometric proportions of earlier Egyptian art. The sun-disk's rays are strictly linear, a direct emblem of divine presence.
