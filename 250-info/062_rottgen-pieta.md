## #62 — Röttgen Pietà

**Late medieval Europe (German Gothic)** · c. 1300–1325 C.E. · Painted wood · Rheinisches Landesmuseum, Bonn, Germany · 34½ in high

### Summary

A **German Gothic Andachtsbild** — a painted wooden **Pietà** deliberately disturbing in its grief, showing an emaciated, wound-torn Christ in a sorrowing Mary's lap — a devotional image for private prayer.

- **Historical Context.** 13th- and 14th-century Christianity, shaped by **Saint Francis of Assisi**'s emphasis on Christ's human suffering, embraced **mysticism** — direct personal encounter with divine pain. Made during an age of plague and hardship, the Pietà images the Late-Gothic **Christus patiens** ("suffering Christ") type, replacing the earlier **Christus triumphans** (triumphant Christ).
- **Function.** An **Andachtsbild** — devotional image for personal meditation. Its deliberately grotesque pathos invited the worshipper to identify with both **Mary's grief** and **Christ's suffering**, drawing closer to God through shared human pain.
- **Content.** Mary holds the lifeless body of Christ across her lap. His body is shockingly **emaciated**; wounds pour thick clots of blood; his head lolls on a spindly neck, disproportionately large. Mary's face is a mask of anguish, not serenity.
- **Form.** **Painted wood** at about 35 in — portable enough for private prayer. Rustic carved surface (not polished like marble), making the horror feel more immediate. The usual triangular Pietà composition is broken by Christ's dangling head. Red blood clots form a disturbing rhythmic **pattern**.

**Vocabulary:** Christus triumphans · Christus patiens · mysticism
