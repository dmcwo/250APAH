## #83 — Hunters in the Snow

**Pieter Bruegel the Elder** · 1565 C.E. · Oil on wood · Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna · 46″ × 63¾″

### Summary

A **secular Northern Renaissance landscape** — one of the first in Western art to give nature the lead role.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in Antwerp/Brussels during the 16th-century **Dutch/Flemish Northern Renaissance**, amid the Reformation's break from overtly religious art. Bruegel was a seminal influence on the Dutch Golden Age. The work comes from an unusually cold stretch of the so-called "Little Ice Age."
- **Function.** One of a cycle of **six Labors of the Months** panels (five survive; others include *Gloomy Day*, *Haymaking*, *Harvesters*, *Return of the Herd*) commissioned by Antwerp merchant Niclaes Jongelinck for his country house. The cycle updates a medieval calendar tradition in a secular, peasant-genre mode.
- **Content.** Three weary hunters trudge home with a single fox through barren snow, bony dogs in tow, toward a village and frozen pond where figures skate and play proto-ice-hockey. Jagged mountains, not native to the Low Countries, loom behind.
- **Form.** "Balcony" / high-angle perspective drops the viewer above the scene; cool muted **grey-white-blue** palette; remarkable atmospheric depth achieved via diminishing scale and aerial perspective. Bare tree trunks and black birds form strong diagonal lines leading the eye into the distance.

**Vocabulary:** oil paint · landscape · Northern European tradition · genre painting · Labor of the Months
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.1
