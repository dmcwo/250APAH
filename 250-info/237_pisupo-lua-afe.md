## #237 — Pisupo Lua Afe (Corned Beef 2000)

**Michel Tuffery (Samoan–Cook Islands–Tahitian–Pākehā New Zealand)** · 1994 C.E. · Flattened tin cans, steel, mixed media · Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa · 1150 × 2170 × 650 mm

### Summary

A life-sized **bull assembled from flattened corned-beef cans**, critiquing globalization's impact on Pacific diets and ecosystems.

- **Historical Context.** **Michel Tuffery** (b. 1966 in Wellington, NZ) draws on his **Samoan, Cook Islands, and Tahitian** heritage. Imported canned food reached Samoa in the 1960s as canned pea soup — **pisupo** — and "pisupo" came to mean all canned foods, especially **corned beef**, which replaced traditional foods and became a required gift at Pacific ceremonies. "Lua afe" means "two thousand." Cattle introduced by colonial powers have also damaged fragile Pacific ecosystems.
- **Function.** **Environmental and cultural critique**. Exposes the health crisis caused by imported processed foods, the loss of local foodways, and the ecological cost of cattle. Can be rolled on hidden wheels into public performances.
- **Content.** A life-sized bull — symbol both of the beef industry and of the cultural import replacing native food. Recycled cans turn colonial trade goods back into art about that trade.
- **Form.** Overlapping, fish-scale tiling of flattened corned-beef tin cans riveted and welded onto a steel armature. Dense, hard, heavy, colorful (red and yellow of the Libby's label). Mobile on concealed wheels.

**Vocabulary:** assemblage
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.4, 2.2
