## #241 — Pure Land

**Mariko Mori (Japan/USA)** · 1998 C.E. · Color photograph on glass · Museum of Contemporary Art, Tokyo · 305 × 610 × 2.2 cm

### Summary

A **techno-Buddhist paradise** — Mariko Mori casts herself as a serene deity floating above the Dead Sea, surrounded by alien musician-bodhisattvas.

- **Historical Context.** **Mariko Mori** (b. 1967 in Tokyo), a former model turned artist, trained in London and New York. *Pure Land* is part of Mori's mid-1990s **Nirvana** series — a 3-D video installation of the same scene whose still-photograph sibling stands alone as an art object. Mori draws from **Pure Land Buddhism**, a sect promising rebirth in Amida Buddha's western paradise. Photographed on site at the Dead Sea — associated with purification in both Shinto and Judaic traditions. Inspired in part by Japanese pop aesthetics, anime, and sleep-paralysis experiences.
- **Function.** Blends **religious icon and science-fiction fantasy**. The larger Nirvana installation immersed viewers in 3-D video and scented wind; the photograph distills that experience into a meditative still moment.
- **Content.** Mori poses as **Kichijoten**, a Japanese goddess of fortune, at center, seated on a lotus. Around her float six cartoonish-alien **bodhisattva musicians**. A Tibetan stupa-cum-spaceship floats at right. Distance is compressed; background figures blur as if rushing toward us.
- **Form.** Large cibachrome photograph laminated on glass. Pastel palette (pinks, mauves, aqua). Digital composite of body photography, CGI characters, and painted digital background.

**Vocabulary:** Tibetan stupa · Nirvana · Amida Buddha · Pure Land Buddhism · Bodhisattvas
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.2, 3.3
