## #104 — George Washington

**Jean-Antoine Houdon** · 1788–1792 C.E. · Marble · Richmond, Virginia · 6′ 2″

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Jean-Antoine_Houdon%27s_George_Washington.jpg/250px-Jean-Antoine_Houdon%27s_George_Washington.jpg" width="240" alt="George Washington">

### Summary

A life-size marble portrait of Washington as citizen-soldier, carved by a French master with unprecedented realism.

- **Historical Context.** Carved after the **American Revolutionary War** in honor of Washington's role. The **Virginia General Assembly** commissioned **Benjamin Harrison V** to hire a French sculptor; 20-year-old Houdon (trained at the Académie Royale, winner of the 1761 Prix de Rome) insisted on marble, not bronze. Unwilling to work from a 2D drawing, he traveled to **America in 1785** to take his own measurements and a bust of Washington. He then completed the piece over four years in Paris. One of the only works of a U.S. hero made by a foreigner — Americans deliberately sought a foreign artist.
- **Function.** Commemorates the Revolutionary War as a "monument of affection and gratitude." Presents Washington as the ideal American — a soldier and government figure who is still one of the people, seen in his casual attire and accompanying cane. A conscious rejection of the robes/regalia conventions of older civilizations.
- **Content.** Washington stands accurately portrayed, his sword not in his hand (non-aggression). At his hip are **thirteen rods (fasces)** symbolizing the unity and strength of the United States. Behind him is a **plow** — referencing Washington returning home to his farm after the war rather than to another position of power.
- **Form.** Reduction-carved marble, exactly 6′ 2″ (Washington's true height), in **contrapposto** looking toward the viewer's left. Common period military uniform; leans casually against a pillar with cane in hand. Considered one of the most accurate depictions of Washington ever made.

**Vocabulary:** fasces · equestrian · duality
