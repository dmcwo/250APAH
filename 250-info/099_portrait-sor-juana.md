## #99 — Portrait of Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz

**Miguel Cabrera** · c. 1750 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Museo Nacional de Historia, Castillo de Chapultepec, Mexico · 41½ × 32½ in

### Summary

A **posthumous devotional portrait** of colonial Mexico's greatest intellectual — nun, poet, scholar, and proto-feminist.

- **Historical Context.** Painted ~55 years after Sor Juana's death (1651–1695). She was a criolla child prodigy who chose the **Jeronymite convent** in Mexico City to continue her studies, since marriage was the only alternative. She wrote plays and poetry and defended women's right to education. Cabrera, New Spain's leading 18th-century painter, worked from earlier portraits since he never met her.
- **Function.** Commemorative portrait commissioned by admirers. Asserts the compatibility of **theology** and **intellectual life** for women — Sor Juana as a model of sanctity and scholarship.
- **Content.** Sor Juana stands in her **library** reading a **work of St. Jerome** (her order's patron), a **rosary** in hand. She wears the Jeronymite habit with a large **escudo de monja** (nun's badge) at her chest depicting the **Annunciation**. Behind her, shelves of books — philosophy, theology, literature, science — testify to her learning.
- **Form.** Realistic oil portrait in a restricted palette (black, white, brown, red). Crisp geometric rendering of books and scholarly instruments; soft, slightly hazy modeling on face and habit.

**Vocabulary:** Jeronymites · escudo de monja · Annunciation
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.2
