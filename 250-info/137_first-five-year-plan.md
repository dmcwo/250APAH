## #137 — Illustration from *The Results of the First Five-Year Plan*

**Varvara Stepanova** (Soviet Constructivism / Russian avant-garde) · 1932 C.E. · Photomontage (illustrated book) · Moscow · 11⅞ × 8⅞ in.

### Summary

A **Constructivist photomontage** turning Stalin's first Five-Year Plan into visual propaganda.

- **Historical Context.** Made in **Moscow** in 1932, just as Stalin's first Five-Year Plan (1928–1932) was declared complete a year ahead of schedule. The Russian avant-garde had, by this point, redirected its experimental formal language toward state-sponsored graphic design and propaganda — art for the masses rather than the bourgeoisie.
- **Function.** Propaganda. The image teaches the Soviet public that the Plan worked, and that gratitude is owed to Lenin and Stalin — visual evidence calibrated to shape opinion and loyalty.
- **Content.** A Lenin portrait at right looks off into the "future"; below him a large crowd celebrates. At left, orators, white **CCCP** banners, and a prominent red numeral **5**. A red electrical tower and red-saturated foreground echo the Soviet flag.
- **Form.** **Photomontage** — multiple photographs sliced and layered at mismatched scale to create dynamism. Reduced palette: black-and-white and sepia photographs against geometric planes of red. Sharp contrasts and scale jumps push the images toward the viewer.

**Vocabulary:** USSR · photomontage · propaganda · Five-Year Plan · avant-garde · Constructivism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4
