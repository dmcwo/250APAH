## #223 — Presentation of Fijian Mats and Tapa Cloths to Queen Elizabeth II

**Fijian artists; photograph/video documentation** · 1953 C.E. (original event); ongoing documentation · Multimedia performance with photographic and filmic documentation · Fiji and archives · Variable

### Summary

A documentary record of the 1953 Fijian ceremonial **presentation of mats (*masi*) and barkcloth tapa** to Queen Elizabeth II during her post-coronation tour — an enactment of Pacific gift-exchange diplomacy.

- **Historical Context.** Queen Elizabeth II toured Fiji in December 1953 as part of her six-month Coronation Commonwealth tour. Fijian chiefs and communities received her with a formal presentation of finely woven pandanus **mats** and barkcloth **masi/tapa** — traditional ceremonial wealth. Fiji was then still a British Crown Colony (it gained independence in 1970). The event was filmed and photographed; the textiles themselves entered the Royal Collection.
- **Function.** The presentation enacts Pacific protocols of **tribute and exchange**: offering mats and tapa binds giver and recipient in a reciprocal relationship. Within colonial politics it simultaneously performed loyalty and asserted the sovereign dignity of Fijian chiefs.
- **Content.** Fijian men and women in ceremonial dress ritually present stacks of woven mats and long rolls of barkcloth to the seated Queen. The textiles are the real "content" — carefully produced women's art patterned with geometric designs specific to Fijian clans and regions.
- **Form.** Documentary still photography and newsreel footage freeze an ephemeral ceremony. The textiles themselves are soft, flexible, sometimes monumental in scale.
