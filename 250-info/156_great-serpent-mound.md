## #156 — Great Serpent Mound

**Mississippian / Fort Ancient culture (Eastern Woodlands)** · c. 1070 C.E. · Earthwork / effigy mound · Adams County, southern Ohio · ~1,300 ft long, 800+ ft of winding body

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/The_Great_Serpent_Mound.jpg/330px-The_Great_Serpent_Mound.jpg" width="240" alt="Great Serpent Mound">

### Summary

The largest surviving **effigy mound** in North America — an 800-foot serpent built into the Ohio landscape.

- **Historical Context.** Two leading attributions: the earlier **Adena culture** (c. 800 B.C.E.) and the later **Fort Ancient culture** (c. 1070 C.E.), with radiocarbon dating favoring the latter. The head of the serpent is aligned with the **summer solstice sunset**, and the curves of its body appear to align with lunar events, implying an astronomical function.
- **Function.** Likely ceremonial, astronomical, or calendrical rather than funerary — no burials are inside the serpent itself, though nearby conical mounds contain burials.
- **Content.** A winding serpent effigy with a coiled tail at one end and an open mouth at the other, appearing to swallow (or release) an oval form.
- **Form.** Yellow-brown clay and ash built up over a rock core and finished with soil, now grassed over. Visible in plan from above; the coiled body is rhythmic and flowing rather than geometric.

**Vocabulary:** effigy mound · earthwork · solstice alignment
