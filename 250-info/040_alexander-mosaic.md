## #40 — Alexander Mosaic from the House of the Faun, Pompeii

**Republican Roman, copy after Greek (Hellenistic) original** · c. 100 B.C.E. (Roman); Greek original c. 310 B.C.E. · Mosaic (tesserae) · National Archaeological Museum, Naples · 8 ft 11 in × 16 ft 9 in

### Summary

A floor **mosaic** from the grandest house in Pompeii that copies a lost Greek painting of the **Battle of Issus** — **Alexander the Great** driving his cavalry through Darius III's lines.

- **Historical Context.** Found in the floor of the **House of the Faun** in Pompeii, preserved by the **Vesuvius eruption of 79 C.E.** The Greek original — possibly by **Philoxenos of Eretria** or **Helen of Egypt** — commemorated **Alexander's victory over Darius III** at Issus (333 B.C.E.), a decisive moment in the collapse of the Persian Empire.
- **Function.** A spectacularly elaborate **floor mosaic** for an elite domus — unusually detailed for something meant to be walked on, clearly made to be admired by guests.
- **Content.** Left: **Alexander** in a cuirass bears down on horseback, young, focused, spear forward. Right: **Darius III**, arms extended, looks back in alarm as his charioteer whips the horses into retreat. Fallen Persian soldiers and a forest of sarissas fill the space.
- **Form.** Dense **opus vermiculatum** ("worm work") in tiny tesserae, dominated by **warm reds, yellows, and browns**. Sophisticated **foreshortening** (notably the horse seen from behind), strong **diagonals**, dramatic chiaroscuro — techniques preserved from a Greek painting.

**Vocabulary:** mosaic · mural · foreshortening · tesserae
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3
