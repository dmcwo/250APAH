## #152 — House in New Castle County

**Robert Venturi, John Rauch, and Denise Scott Brown** (Postmodernism) · 1978–1983 C.E. · Wood frame and stucco · New Castle County, Delaware

### Summary

A **Postmodernist** house that rejects the severe International Style for reference and wit.

- **Historical Context.** Designed in northern Delaware by **Venturi, Rauch and Scott Brown**, the architects whose book *Learning from Las Vegas* launched American **Postmodern** architecture. A direct repudiation of the stripped-down modernism of Mies, Corbusier, and their heirs.
- **Function.** A private house for a family of three — a musician wife and a birdwatcher husband — with rooms shaped around their specific interests.
- **Content.** Exterior deliberately awkward and whimsical; painted **white to reference American barn vernacular**; centered on a large fireplace inside, with rooms in unexpected and irregular scales.
- **Form.** Wood frame and stucco; traditional American sources filtered through a Postmodern lens — complex, contradictory, ornamented, full of historical references, with surprising interior volumes.

**Vocabulary:** Postmodernism · vernacular · contradiction
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.2, 2.3
