## #190 — The Court of Gayumars, folio from Shah Tahmasp's *Shahnama*

**Sultan Muhammad** (Safavid Persia) · 1522–1525 C.E. · Ink, opaque watercolor, and gold on paper · Tabriz, Iran

### Summary

A **Safavid** folio widely considered the single finest Persian miniature ever painted.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by the first **Safavid** shah, **Isma'il I**, and completed under his son **Tahmasp I**. The Safavids, claiming descent from Sufi shaikhs, sponsored the arts on a grand scale — this particular *Shahnama* (Book of Kings) manuscript is the most ambitious of the genre. **Gayumars** is the mythic first king of Iran; his son **Siyamak** will soon be murdered by the demon **Ahriman**'s son, Black Div.
- **Function.** Folio from a royally-commissioned manuscript that recorded all Persian kings to legitimize the Safavid dynasty and to educate royal heirs on ideal kingship.
- **Content.** King Gayumars enthroned on a **tiger-skin** throne at the top of a mountain landscape; his court, dressed in leopard pelts, forms a **semicircle** around him. **Siyamak** sits to his left; grandson **Hushang** to his right. An angel, **Surush**, appears to deliver the prophecy of Siyamak's murder. A garden spreads in front, a mountain rises behind — everyone is peaceful before the coming tragedy.
- **Form.** **Ink, opaque watercolor, and gold on paper**. Composition built around a radial semicircle. Faces and rock-cloud shapes show strong **Chinese / East Asian** influence absorbed via the Mongol period. Overall harmony between figures and landscape; no single detail overwhelms — a hallmark of Sultan Muhammad's style.

**Vocabulary:** folio · illuminated manuscript · Shahnama · Safavid
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.3, 3.5
