## #209 — Taj Mahal

**Mughal India (patron: Shah Jahan; chief architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahauri)** · 1632–1653 C.E. · Stone masonry and marble with inlay of precious and semiprecious stones; gardens · Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India · Mausoleum ~240 ft tall on 1,902 × 1,002 ft complex

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Taj_Mahal%2C_Agra%2C_India_edit3.jpg/500px-Taj_Mahal%2C_Agra%2C_India_edit3.jpg" width="240" alt="Taj Mahal">

### Summary

A white-marble mausoleum built by **Shah Jahan for his wife Mumtaz Mahal** — widely regarded as the pinnacle of Mughal architecture and a "paradise on Earth."

- **Historical Context.** **Shah Jahan**, fifth Mughal emperor, commissioned the mausoleum after **Mumtaz Mahal** died in childbirth in 1631. Some 20,000 laborers and craftsmen worked for two decades; materials came from across the empire and beyond — white marble from Makrana (Rajasthan), jade from China, lapis from Afghanistan. Shah Jahan was later deposed by his son Aurangzeb and imprisoned at Agra Fort, where he could see the Taj across the river.
- **Function.** **Tomb** for Mumtaz Mahal (and, later, Shah Jahan himself — he was never able to build his rumored matching black tomb). A congregational mosque stands to the west of the mausoleum; a matching "answer" building mirrors it to the east.
- **Content.** Central tomb chamber holds the cenotaphs of Mumtaz and Shah Jahan; the actual sarcophagi are in a crypt below. Exterior bears pietra-dura floral inlays and Qur'anic inscriptions in black stone calligraphy. Set within a **charbagh** (four-part paradise garden) bisected by watercourses at the end of a long reflecting pool.
- **Form.** Central bulbous dome with four **minarets** anchoring the corners of the marble plinth. Perfectly symmetrical; the mausoleum is offset to the end of the garden rather than its center, so the river becomes part of the composition. Iwans (arched recesses) pierce each face.

**Vocabulary:** Mughal Empire · minaret · cenotaph · charbagh · pietra dura
