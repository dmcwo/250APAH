## #73 — Last Supper

**Leonardo da Vinci** · c. 1494–1498 C.E. · Oil and tempera on plaster · Santa Maria delle Grazie, Milan · 15 ft × 29 ft

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/%C3%9Altima_Cena_-_Da_Vinci_5.jpg/500px-%C3%9Altima_Cena_-_Da_Vinci_5.jpg" width="240" alt="The Last Supper">

### Summary

Leonardo's **psychologically charged** rendering of the moment Christ announces his betrayal.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by Duke **Ludovico Sforza** of Milan for the refectory (dining hall) of the Dominican convent of **Santa Maria delle Grazie**. Painted during the High Renaissance; Leonardo's experimental oil-and-tempera-on-dry-plaster technique caused the work to deteriorate within decades.
- **Function.** A refectory mural situating the friars' own meals beneath Christ's final meal. It structures the room as an extension of the painted space.
- **Content.** Christ sits at the center of a long table; the twelve apostles react in shock to the words "one of you will betray me," arranged in four groups of three. Judas clutches a money bag and recoils into shadow.
- **Form.** Strict **one-point linear perspective** with the vanishing point at Christ's temple; symmetrical architecture and a pyramidal Christ anchor the composition. Each apostle is individuated through gesture and expression — a study in psychological realism.

**Vocabulary:** linear perspective · fresco secco · High Renaissance
