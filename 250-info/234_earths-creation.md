## #234 — Earth's Creation I

**Emily Kame Kngwarreye (Anmatyerre, Australia)** · 1994 C.E. · Synthetic polymer paint (acrylic) on canvas · Private collection · 275 × 632 cm (set of four panels)

### Summary

A monumental Aboriginal painting of the **Dreaming** rendered as lush abstract dabs — at the time of its 2007 sale, the most expensive painting by a female Australian artist.

- **Historical Context.** **Emily Kame Kngwarreye** (c. 1910–1996) was a senior Anmatyerre woman of the **Utopia community** in Australia's Northern Territory. She began painting on canvas only at about age 80, after decades of practicing **awelye** (women's ceremonial body painting) and batik. In less than a decade she produced roughly 3,000 paintings and became internationally recognized. *Earth's Creation* relates to the rainy season and the emergence of life from the desert after storms — a vivid moment of the **Dreaming** (Tjukurrpa), the ancestral creative force in Aboriginal cosmology.
- **Function.** A **ceremonial and ancestral painting** rooted in *awelye*. It embodies the artist's custodial knowledge of her Country — specifically the yam (*anooralya*) and pencil-yam (*kame*) Dreaming for which she was named.
- **Content.** Non-figurative: dense, overlapping layered dabs of bright color — greens, yellows, pinks, reds — evoke the desert landscape springing to life after rain. The title refers to the primordial moment of the world's making.
- **Form.** Four large panels totaling more than 20 feet wide. Thick, rapid layering of paint applied with a stippling, dabbing technique in a dense, all-over composition. No horizon, no figure — an aerial/spiritual view of country.

**Vocabulary:** Aboriginal Dreaming (Tjukurrpa) · awelye · Utopia community
