## #80 — Venus of Urbino

**Titian** · c. 1538 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Uffizi, Florence · 119 × 165 cm

### Summary

A reclining female nude whose **pointed domestic setting** transforms a classical Venus into a marital allegory.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by **Guidobaldo II della Rovere**, Duke of Urbino, likely on the occasion of his marriage to Giulia Varano. Titian, the leading Venetian painter, drew on Giorgione's *Sleeping Venus* (c. 1510), which he may have helped complete, but brought the goddess indoors and awake.
- **Function.** A private bedroom painting — an *allegory of marriage* celebrating fidelity, fertility, and sensual love. The dog at the feet symbolizes fidelity; the *cassoni* (marriage chests) in the background allude to dowry and domestic virtue.
- **Content.** A nude woman reclines on a rumpled bed in a Venetian palazzo interior; she gazes directly at the viewer, left hand covering her pudenda, right hand holding roses. A maid and a young girl rummage in *cassoni* in the background; a small dog sleeps.
- **Form.** Warm Venetian **colorito** — glowing flesh, rich reds, deep greens. Strong diagonal of the body cutting across the picture plane. Space divided by a vertical curtain into near/far zones. Later referenced by Manet's *Olympia* (#115).

**Vocabulary:** Venetian Renaissance · colorito · reclining nude · cassoni
