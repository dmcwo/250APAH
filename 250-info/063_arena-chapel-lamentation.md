## #63 — Arena (Scrovegni) Chapel, including Lamentation

**Giotto di Bondone (artist); unknown architect** · Chapel c. 1303 C.E.; fresco c. 1305 · Brick (architecture) and fresco · Padua, Italy · *Lamentation* fresco 7 ft 7 in × 7 ft 9 in

### Summary

A small **Proto-Renaissance** chapel in Padua covered in **Giotto's** fresco cycle — a turning point in Western painting where **weight, space, and human emotion** enter medieval religious art.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by **Enrico Scrovegni**, a wealthy Paduan banker, partly as atonement for his father's (and his own) sin of **usury** — charging interest was considered sinful. Built on the site of an ancient Roman arena. Marks the **Late Gothic / Proto-Renaissance** moment around 1300, when humanist thought and naturalistic depiction began to reshape religious art.
- **Function.** A private **family chapel** for the Scrovegni, dedicated to the **Virgin of Charity**. The fresco cycle served both as personal atonement and as instruction for illiterate worshippers ("picture-Bible" function).
- **Content.** A complete cycle of **38 scenes** from the lives of Mary and Christ arranged in three tiers; the **Last Judgment** covers the west wall and the **Annunciation** frames the chancel. In the *Lamentation* scene (image set focus), mourners grieve over Christ's body — expressions range from stoic to convulsed; angels wheel in the sky; a barren rocky diagonal leads the eye to the heads of Christ and Mary.
- **Form.** **Buon fresco** on plaster over brick. **Modeled volumes** (bodies feel heavy; drapery falls over real limbs); sparse but believable architectural space; attention to **individualized emotion**. Halos preserve divine status in otherwise humanized figures.

**Vocabulary:** fresco · Late Gothic · Proto-Renaissance
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 2.1
