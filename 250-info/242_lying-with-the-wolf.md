## #242 — Lying with the Wolf

**Kiki Smith (USA)** · 2001 C.E. · Ink and pencil on paper · Private collection · 72 × 88 in

### Summary

**Kiki Smith**'s large drawing of a nude woman reclining peacefully with a wolf — a revision of the predator-prey dynamic of Red Riding Hood and a feminist reimagining of St. Genevieve.

- **Historical Context.** **Kiki Smith** (b. 1954, Nuremberg; raised in New Jersey), trained as an EMT before becoming an artist. Her works are centered on the body, folklore, and feminism. *Lying with the Wolf* is part of a series reworking fairy tales (especially Little Red Riding Hood) and female saints (St. Genevieve, who was said to have tamed wolves). Drawn in the wake of decades of feminist art theory challenging male depictions of the female nude.
- **Function.** Reverses the expected narrative of female victimhood. In place of the wolf devouring the girl, Smith's woman embraces the wolf calmly — a meditation on **reconciliation between humanity and the wild, between woman and the "beast."**
- **Content.** A life-sized, naked woman reclines on the floor holding a large wolf in a near-embrace. Both are rendered in warm, sensitive contour lines. Calm, meditative, intimate — neither menacing nor erotic.
- **Form.** Large-format drawing in ink and pencil on multiple joined sheets of Nepalese paper. Minimal ground; figures outlined with graceful line and soft tonal shading. Scale approaches that of a history painting.

**Vocabulary:** feminist art · folklore revision · St. Genevieve
