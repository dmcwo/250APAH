## #46 — Pantheon

**Imperial Roman** · 118–125 C.E. · Concrete with stone facing · Rome, Italy · 142 ft high dome, 142 ft interior diameter, 150 ft overall height

### Summary

**Hadrian's Pantheon** — a temple "to all the gods" whose **coffered concrete dome** and **oculus** remain the largest unreinforced concrete dome ever built, and define the possibilities of Roman architecture.

- **Historical Context.** Completed under **Hadrian** on the site of an earlier temple built by Marcus Agrippa (hence the surviving inscription). Designed by **Apollodorus of Damascus**. Converted into a **Christian church** (Santa Maria ad Martyres) in 609 C.E. — which is why it survives.
- **Function.** A temple **to all the gods** — a political gesture that folded traditional Roman religion into the imperial order. Later a church and pilgrimage site; today also a museum.
- **Content.** A rectangular **portico** with 16 granite Corinthian columns fronts a vast circular **rotunda** topped by a **hemispherical coffered dome**. At the apex, a 27-ft **oculus** admits sun and rain.
- **Form.** The building is essentially a sphere inscribed in a cylinder: the interior width equals the dome's height. Poured **concrete** graded from heavy aggregate at the base to light volcanic pumice near the oculus; five rings of **coffers** reduce weight and embellish the inner dome. Marble and porphyry **floor** patterns continue the geometry below.

**Vocabulary:** Corinthian columns · oculus · coffers · coffered dome · portico · barrel vaults · rotunda · vestibule · pediment / tympanum · dome
