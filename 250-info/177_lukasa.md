## #177 — Lukasa (memory board)

**Mbudye Society, Luba peoples** (Democratic Republic of the Congo) · c. 19th–20th century C.E. · Wood, beads, and metal · 54.8 × 21 cm

### Summary

A handheld **mnemonic device** read by touch to recall Luba royal history and cosmology.

- **Historical Context.** The **Luba Kingdom** was a powerful presence in central Africa from the 16th to the 20th centuries. Within Luba society the **Mbudye** were the "men of memory" — an elite association who preserved royal histories, cosmology, and political knowledge through embodied practice.
- **Function.** A **memory aid**, not a text. A Mbudye diviner held the board in the left hand and "read" it with the right index finger — the configuration of beads, shells, and incised patterns cued each recounting of origin myths, king lists, sacred enclosures, and royal relationships.
- **Content.** Hourglass-shaped wooden tablet covered on one side with colored beads, shells, and metal fragments in clusters; geometric carvings on the back and sides. Large beads surrounded by smaller beads stand for **culture heroes**; circles of beads for sacred enclosures; lines and clusters for events and relationships.
- **Form.** Rough-textured wood, small enough to hold in one hand. Multicolored beads pop against the darker wood background. Geometric designs incised on the reverse.

**Vocabulary:** Lukasa · Mbudye Society · mnemonic
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 2.1, 2.2
