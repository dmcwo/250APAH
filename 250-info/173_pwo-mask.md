## #173 — Female (Pwo) mask

**Chokwe peoples** (Democratic Republic of the Congo / Angola) · Late 19th–early 20th century C.E. · Wood, fiber, pigment, and metal · 39.1 × 21.3 × 23.5 cm

### Summary

A Chokwe mask embodying the **idealized female ancestor** — worn by men in masquerade performance.

- **Historical Context.** The **Chokwe** are a matrilineal people of the DRC and Angola who honor young fertile women. The mask represents the **founding female ancestor** of the Chokwe lineage and is carved by a Chokwe man for the male dancer who performs the role.
- **Function.** Worn in masquerade performances to honor women who have passed through childbirth and retain wisdom and beauty; tied to coming-of-age rituals and to the veneration of the female ancestor.
- **Content.** A calm, closed-eyed, closed-mouthed female face with **scarification patterns**, a narrow chin, and a wide forehead. The closed eyes and mouth signal respect and inner knowledge; **white kaolin** around the eyes signals the spirit realm and the female capacity for second sight granted by childbirth. The fashionable hairstyle of the period is modeled on top.
- **Form.** Carved wood oiled to a smooth sheen for contrast against the softer, textured fiber hair and rougher headband. Idealized rather than individualized; symmetrical; small features (eyes, ears, mouth) symbolize the virtues of discretion.

**Vocabulary:** Pwo · matrilineal culture · Chihongo · kaolin
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.2
