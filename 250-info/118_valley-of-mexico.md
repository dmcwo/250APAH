## #118 — The Valley of Mexico from the Hillside of Santa Isabel (El Valle de México desde el Cerro de Santa Isabel)

**José María Velasco** · 1882 C.E. · Oil on canvas

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e2/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Velasco_-_The_Valley_of_Mexico_from_the_Santa_Isabel_Mountain_Range_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" width="240" alt="The Valley of Mexico from the Hillside of Santa Isabel">

### Summary

A sweeping **Neoclassical Mexican landscape** painted by Velasco in his characteristic naturalist-scientific manner.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in Mexico. Velasco was the preeminent Mexican landscape painter of the later 19th century; the student notes place the work in a tradition of continuity rather than rupture.
- **Function.** A landscape celebrating place — it focuses on environmental elements rather than city life.
- **Content.** A scene of a woman with her children and their dog walking along the valley; abundant vegetation is shown across a vast landscape depicting the entire valley.
- **Form.** Oil on canvas; carefully observed natural detail paired with broad panoramic composition.
