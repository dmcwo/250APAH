## #26 — Athenian Agora

**Archaic through Hellenistic Greek** · 600 B.C.E.–150 C.E. · Marble (plan) · Athens, Greece · ~397,000 sq ft (excavated)

### Summary

The civic and commercial heart of ancient Athens — a multi-purpose **public square** that fused market, courthouse, temple, and philosophical arena, threaded by the **Panathenaic Way**.

- **Historical Context.** Classical Athens grew wealthy on maritime trade and Delian League tribute; the agora's scale and furnishings reflect that prosperity. The site was devastated by the Persian invasion and rebuilt in subsequent centuries.
- **Function.** All-purpose civic center: **market**, political meeting ground, **philosophical forum** (Socrates famously interrogated passers-by here), mint, and law court. Citizens gathered to **vote**; temples and altars were distributed throughout.
- **Content.** A large open area ringed by stoas, temples, government buildings, and workshops. The **Panathenaic Way** cuts diagonally through the agora toward the **Acropolis**. Structures on-site included the Tholos, the Bouleuterion, and various stoas (including the restored Stoa of Attalos).
- **Form.** Largely a **planned open square** rather than a single building — buildings in **marble** and stone, post-and-lintel construction, integrated around public circulation.

**Vocabulary:** colonnade · column · façade · peristyle
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.4, 2.2, 3.1
