## #136 — Composition with Red, Blue and Yellow

**Piet Mondrian** (De Stijl / Neoplasticism) · 1930 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Paris · 18.11 × 18.11 in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Piet_Mondriaan%2C_1930_-_Mondrian_Composition_II_in_Red%2C_Blue%2C_and_Yellow.jpg/330px-Piet_Mondriaan%2C_1930_-_Mondrian_Composition_II_in_Red%2C_Blue%2C_and_Yellow.jpg" width="240" alt="Composition with Red, Blue and Yellow">

### Summary

The pure **Neoplastic** grid — primary colors, black lines, and nothing else.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in Paris by **Piet Mondrian**, the Dutch co-founder with **Theo van Doesburg** of **De Stijl** ("The Style"), a utopian art movement born out of WWI disillusionment that sought spiritual harmony through pure abstraction. Mondrian had moved from the Netherlands to Paris in 1918 and remained there until 1938. By 1930 De Stijl was already declining.
- **Function.** Non-representational. The painting has no subject; it aims only to embody a universal cosmic order — spiritual harmony expressed in the simplest possible visual language.
- **Content.** Variations of squares and rectangles filled with the primary colors (red, blue, yellow) against white, bounded by thick black horizontal and vertical lines. A large red square dominates the upper right.
- **Form.** Oil on canvas. Looks flat when reproduced but the actual surface has texture — the whites are layered with visible directional brushwork while the blacks and primary colors are flatter. The composition is asymmetric yet balanced.

**Vocabulary:** Neoplasticism · De Stijl · abstraction · primary colors
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.3, 3.1
