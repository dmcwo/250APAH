## #200 — Lakshmana Temple

**Chandela Dynasty (patron: Yashovarman)** · 930–950 C.E. · Sandstone · Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh, India · ~98 ft long and tall

### Summary

A Nagara-style **Hindu temple-mountain** dedicated to Vishnu, famed for its dense figural sculpture including the frank erotic reliefs of Khajuraho.

- **Historical Context.** Part of the **Khajuraho Group of Monuments**, built between 950–1050 C.E. by the **Chandela Dynasty** during a Hindu resurgence in north-central India. Yashovarman commissioned the Lakshmana Temple to house a sacred image of Vaikuntha Vishnu received from a Pratihara overlord, using the temple to proclaim his dynasty's legitimacy and power.
- **Function.** Housed the Vishnu image in the innermost sanctuary (**garbhagriha**) and served as a pilgrimage destination. Devotees circumambulated the temple and its enclosed sanctum.
- **Content.** Multiple sculpted bands wrap the exterior: deities, celestial nymphs (surasundaris), couples in ordinary and erotic embraces (mithunas), mythic animals. The central shrine holds a four-headed Vishnu. Four subsidiary shrines mark the corners of the raised platform.
- **Form.** Nagara-style "mountain-peak" **shikhara** (tower) rising in a cluster of smaller replica peaks (**urushringas**), symbolizing Mount Meru. Axially planned: entrance porch → mandapa (hall) → antarala → garbhagriha. Densely carved exterior; dark, austere sanctum interior.

**Vocabulary:** Hinduism · dharma · sanctum · mandapa · garbhagriha · shikhara · urushringa · tirtha · Vishnu · Brahma
