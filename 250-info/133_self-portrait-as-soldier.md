## #133 — Self-Portrait as a Soldier

**Ernst Ludwig Kirchner** (Die Brücke / German Expressionism) · 1915 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Allen Memorial Art Museum, Oberlin College, Ohio · 24 × 27 in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Kirchner_-_Selbstbildnis_als_Soldat.jpg/500px-Kirchner_-_Selbstbildnis_als_Soldat.jpg" width="240" alt="Self-Portrait as a Soldier">

### Summary

An anti-war **Expressionist** self-portrait painted during Kirchner's breakdown in WWI.

- **Historical Context.** Kirchner served briefly as a driver in **WWI** before being discharged within months for partial paralysis, drug and alcohol addiction, weak lungs, and collapsing mental health. Co-founder of **Die Brücke** ("The Bridge"), the 1905–1913 German Expressionist group that merged Primitivism with modernist avant-garde. The painting was later seized and displayed in the Nazi **Degenerate Art Exhibition (1937)** as "Soldier with Whore"; Kirchner committed suicide in Switzerland in 1938.
- **Function.** A public expression of Kirchner's disillusionment, psychological damage, and fear that the war had ended him as an artist.
- **Content.** Kirchner stands in uniform at the foreground, eyes blank blue, **right hand severed** — though he never lost his hand, the amputation symbolizes fear he could no longer paint. Behind him, a gaunt, androgynous naked model (possibly a symbolic self-reflection) sits with empty black eyes. The model also seems to blur into a painting on the wall.
- **Form.** Oil on canvas; chaotic composition; saturated red background, black canvas behind the model, green wall matching his severed stump; figures rendered with elongated, jaundiced-yellow bodies. All colors are vivid and disorganized.

**Vocabulary:** Primitivism · Expressionism · Die Brücke
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 3.2
