## #11 — Terra Cotta Fragment

**Lapita culture** · c. 1000 B.C.E. · Clay · Solomon Islands / Reef Islands (Pacific)

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/PortVilaLapita.jpg/250px-PortVilaLapita.jpg" width="240" alt="Lapita pottery fragment">

### Summary

A shattered **Lapita** ceramic piece notable for its stylized human face among geometric bands — early evidence of the design tradition that would shape Micro- and Polynesian art.

- **Historical Context.** Made by the **Lapita culture**, ancestors of the Micronesian and Polynesian peoples who dispersed across a vast network of Pacific islands.
- **Function.** Uncertain because of the fragment's condition. Likely pottery, but Lapita terra-cotta forms may also have served as **stamps** to imprint patterns onto bark cloth or human skin for tattoos.
- **Content.** A stylized **human head** set among dense, incised geometric patterns. Lapita burial custom of separating heads from bodies (replaced by cone-shell rings) suggests religious importance placed on the head.
- **Form.** Dense, repeating **geometric ornament** framing an abstracted face; flat, stylized, low-relief incision.

**Vocabulary:** stylization · abstraction · ornamental
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.4, 3.1
