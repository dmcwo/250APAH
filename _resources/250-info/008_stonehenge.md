## #8 — Stonehenge

**Neolithic Britons** · c. 2500–1600 B.C.E. · Sandstone (sarsen) and bluestone · Wiltshire, UK · outer ring ~97 ft diameter; tallest stone 24 ft

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Stonehenge2007_07_30.jpg/250px-Stonehenge2007_07_30.jpg" width="240" alt="Stonehenge">

### Summary

A Neolithic-to-Bronze-Age **megalithic monument** whose post-and-lintel ring was built in phases over nearly a millennium.

- **Historical Context.** Built across about 1,000 years in **Wiltshire, England**, spanning the transition from stone tools to Bronze Age metallurgy. Nearby **round barrows** (burial mounds) and the lavishly furnished **Amesbury Archer** grave mark a shift to individual burial with grave goods. Later cultures — Celts, Anglo-Saxons (one decapitated man buried there), medieval chroniclers crediting **Merlin** — reused and reinterpreted the site.
- **Function.** Unknown and probably multiple. Evidence of **cremation burials** in the Aubrey holes points to funerary use; the alignment of the trilithon horseshoe with the **midsummer sunrise and midwinter sunset** points to an astronomical/ceremonial calendar function.
- **Content.** A circular bank and ditch (~320 ft across) enclosing an outer ring of **sarsen post-and-lintel trilithons** and an inner horseshoe of five larger trilithons; smaller **bluestones** set between and within.
- **Form.** Massive sarsen sandstone blocks (25–50 tons each) capped by horizontal **lintels** locked with mortise-and-tenon joints — the structure's defining **post-and-lintel** system. Bluestones brought from as far as the **Preseli Hills in Wales** (~150 miles away).

**Vocabulary:** megalith · trilithon · henge · post · lintel
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.4, 3.3, 3.4
