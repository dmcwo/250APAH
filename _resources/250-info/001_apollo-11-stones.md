## #1 — Apollo 11 Stones

**Early Homo Sapiens (San peoples)** · c. 23,000 B.C.E. · Charcoal on slabs of rock · Apollo 11 Caves, Namibia · ~4.25 × 5 in

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Apollo-11-cave-therianthrope.jpg/250px-Apollo-11-cave-therianthrope.jpg" width="240" alt="Apollo 11 Stones">

### Summary

Among the **oldest known figurative artworks in Africa** — portable stone slabs depicting animals.

- **Historical Context.** Found in the **Apollo 11 Caves, Namibia**, and named by German archaeologist Wolfgang Wendt after the 1969 moon landing. Attributed to early Homo sapiens (ancestral to the **San peoples**). Carbon-dated roughly 25,000–27,000 years old, among the earliest figurative art yet found on the continent.
- **Function.** Unknown, but likely ritual or mnemonic; the animal images may have recorded species of importance to hunter-gatherers, or served a shamanic/identification purpose.
- **Content.** Seven small slabs carrying charcoal traces of animal figures — a feline-like creature with human hind legs is the most famous, suggesting an early **therianthrope** (human-animal hybrid).
- **Form.** Pigments of black charcoal, reddish ochre, and white kaolin on portable stone. Outlined animal forms use **pictorial definition** — the clearest identifying silhouette rather than naturalistic shading.

**Vocabulary:** prehistory · cave painting · conceptual realism · pictorial definition
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.4, 3.1
