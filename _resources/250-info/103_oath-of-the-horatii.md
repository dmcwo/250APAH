## #103 — The Oath of the Horatii

**Jacques-Louis David** (Revolutionary and Napoleonic France) · 1784 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Musée du Louvre, Paris · 129.8 × 167.2 in

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Le_Serment_des_Horaces_-_Jacques-Louis_David_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3692_%3B_MR_1432.jpg/500px-Le_Serment_des_Horaces_-_Jacques-Louis_David_-_Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre_Peintures_INV_3692_%3B_MR_1432.jpg" width="240" alt="The Oath of the Horatii">

### Summary

The founding manifesto of **French Neoclassicism** — a call to civic virtue on the eve of the French Revolution.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in late 18th-century France as artists were turning away from **Rococo**. David trained first under the Rococo painter **Boucher**, then under **Joseph-Marie Vien**. He went to Italy in 1775, filling 12 sketchbooks and studying Raphael; saw the ruins of **Pompeii** in 1779. Commissioned by **Louis XVI** in 1784; unveiled in David's Rome studio in 1785, then at the **Paris Salon**. David drew on **Poussin**'s *Rape of the Sabine Women* for the lictor figure.
- **Function.** An **exemplum virtutis** celebrating stoic patriotism — the brothers' restrained emotion, order, and patriotic duty supported the rising republican call to sacrifice for the state. Interpreted in the context of the approaching French Revolution.
- **Content.** The story from **Livy**: the three Horatii brothers pledge to their father to fight the Curiatii of Alba Longa to the death, representing Rome. The women — intermarried with the Curiatii — slump in grief at right, knowing they will lose either way. Three swords in the father's raised left hand; the sons' arms salute rigidly.
- **Form.** Life-size figures arranged as a frieze in the foreground; three sculptural groups placed before three arches in a Roman atrium. Men built from straight lines, vivid colors, geometric tension; women rendered in **sinuous** curves and muted tones. Stark, clinical light; space cut off by the arches in the manner of Roman relief sculpture.

**Vocabulary:** Atrium · exemplum virtutis · lictor · Salon · sinuous
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4, 2.2
