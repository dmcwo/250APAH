## #155 — Yaxchilán

**Maya** (Late Classic) · 725 C.E. · Limestone (architectural complex and relief lintels) · Chiapas, Mexico · Lintel 25 ~51 × 34 in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Yaxchilan_lintel_25.jpg/250px-Yaxchilan_lintel_25.jpg" width="240" alt="Yaxchilán Lintel 25">

### Summary

A Late Classic **Maya** city famous for its carved limestone **lintels** documenting royal blood-ritual.

- **Historical Context.** Yaxchilán is a Maya city on the Usumacinta River in Chiapas, Mexico. The most famous images are the lintels from **Structure 23**, commissioned by king **Shield Jaguar II** and dedicated to his wife **Lady K'abal Xook (Lady Xook)** to commemorate his accession to the throne.
- **Function.** The lintels, hung over doorways, publicly proclaimed royal legitimacy. **Bloodletting** was a central Maya royal rite — a portal through which ancestors and gods could be called — and tied Shield Jaguar's reign to the creation of humans from the gods' blood.
- **Content.** **Lintel 25** shows Lady Xook kneeling below a rising **Vision Serpent** that emerges from a basin of bloodied paper. A warrior-ancestor emerges from the serpent's mouth; the Mesoamerican rain-and-war god **Tlaloc** appears from its other head. **Lintel 24** shows her pulling a thorned cord through her tongue in the act of bloodletting itself. The stairs carry hieroglyphic writing and stelae.
- **Form.** High-quality **limestone low relief**. Dense, geometric compositions with intricate detail in headdresses, jewelry, and text; symmetrical and balanced; carefully positioned negative space.

**Vocabulary:** lintel · Lady Xook · Shield Jaguar · bloodletting · Vision Serpent
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4
