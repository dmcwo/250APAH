## #6 — Anthropomorphic Stele

**Unknown pre-Islamic Arabian culture** · Fourth millennium B.C.E. · Sandstone · Found near Ha'il, northwest Saudi Arabia · ~3 ft tall

### Summary

A **pre-Islamic Arabian grave marker** in low relief, human in shape but sharply geometric.

- **Historical Context.** Found in a small village near **Ha'il, northwestern Saudi Arabia**. Two similar steles are known in the region — shared features suggest contact between mobile groups as the Arabian savanna was drying into desert and hunter-gatherers shifted toward domestication. The Arabian Peninsula was a **commercial crossroads** between East and West through the Bronze Age and Antiquity, which helps explain the stele family's shared iconography.
- **Function.** Likely **funerary** — a grave marker in an open-air sanctuary, thought to capture the essence of the deceased.
- **Content.** Simplified human figure seen only from the front: trapezoidal head with closely spaced eyes and a flat nose, square shoulders, a **necklace with an awl** across the chest, and a **double-bladed dagger hanging from a belt** at the waist — the weaponry pointing to an elite male.
- **Form.** Carved from a single slab of sandstone in **low relief**; never painted. Stiff outer contours, simple curved interior features. Frontal, motionless, abstract — recognizably human without any naturalistic modeling.

**Vocabulary:** anthropomorphic · stele · low relief
