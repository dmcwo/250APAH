## #213 — Nan Madol

**Saudeleur Dynasty, Pohnpei (Micronesia)** · c. 700–1600 C.E. · Basalt boulders and prismatic columns · Pohnpei Island, Micronesia · ~0.29 sq mi; walls up to 25 ft tall

### Summary

A city of nearly **100 stone-built islets** in a tidal lagoon — the ritual and political capital of the Saudeleur Dynasty.

- **Historical Context.** Built on and around Temwen Island by the **Saudeleur Dynasty**, which unified Pohnpei c. 1100 C.E. and ruled until c. 1628. The construction required quarrying and rafting prismatic basalt columns from the other side of the island without metal tools or wheeled transport. After the Saudeleur fall to the semi-mythical conqueror Isokelekel, the city gradually depopulated.
- **Function.** A combined **royal, religious, and mortuary complex**: elite residences, priestly quarters, food preparation islands, and tombs for Saudeleur rulers. Its watery channels and tidal causeways controlled access and reinforced hierarchy.
- **Content.** Nearly 100 artificial islets connected by canals; the central royal tomb complex, **Nandauwas**, has walls 25 ft high. Individual islets served specialized functions (meetinghouse, priests, anointing rites).
- **Form.** Massive log-cabin-style walls of naturally prismatic columnar basalt stacked in **header-and-stretcher courses**, filled with coral rubble, raising platforms above tidal waters.

**Vocabulary:** header courses · stretcher courses · megalith · nahnmwarki · Pohnpei
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.1
