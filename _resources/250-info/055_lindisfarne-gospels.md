## #55 — Lindisfarne Gospels: St. Matthew, cross-carpet page; St. Luke portrait page; St. Luke incipit page

**Early medieval (Hiberno-Saxon / Insular) Europe; attributed to Bishop Eadfrith** · c. 700 C.E. · Illuminated manuscript (ink, pigments, and gold on vellum) · Lindisfarne Priory, Northumbria, England · 365 × 275 mm

### Summary

A lavishly illuminated gospel book made at the island monastery of **Lindisfarne** — a synthesis of **Irish (Celtic), Anglo-Saxon, and Mediterranean** artistic traditions in the **Hiberno-Saxon** (Insular) style.

- **Historical Context.** Produced at **Lindisfarne Priory** in Northumbria, traditionally attributed to **Bishop Eadfrith**. Honors **Saint Cuthbert**, whose body was buried there and whose cult drew pilgrims. The book fuses **Italian** (Mediterranean) classical painting traditions with **Celtic interlace** and **Anglo-Saxon animal-style** metalwork ornament.
- **Function.** A **deluxe gospel book** for the altar, used in Christian liturgy and as an object of veneration. Originally had a jeweled **cumdach** (book-shrine) cover added in the 8th century; both outer covers are now lost.
- **Content.** Contains the gospels of **Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John** with canon tables, evangelist portraits, "cross-carpet" pages (full-page crosses), and elaborate **incipit** (opening-letter) pages.
- **Form.** **Illuminated manuscript**: ink, pigments, and gold on **vellum**. Dense **interlace**, zoomorphic knots, and spiral ornament derived from metalwork; Evangelist portraits in a flatter Byzantine-influenced figure style. Integration of figure, pattern, and script unique to Insular art.

**Vocabulary:** illuminated manuscript · insular · Hiberno-Saxon
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.3, 2.1, 2.2
