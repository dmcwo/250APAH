## #37 — Winged Victory of Samothrace

**Hellenistic Greek** · c. 190 B.C.E. · Parian marble · Louvre, Paris · ~9 ft tall

### Summary

**Nike** alighting on the prow of a stone ship — a dramatic **Hellenistic** masterpiece of wet drapery and spiral composition, commemorating a naval victory at the **Sanctuary of the Great Gods on Samothrace**.

- **Historical Context.** Made in the **Hellenistic** era, after Alexander the Great's death, when **Rhodes** and Pergamon competed as artistic centers. The statue shows Rhodian influence and probably commemorates a **Rhodian naval victory** — either at Myonnisos or Side (190 B.C.E.) over Antiochus III.
- **Function.** A **votive** monument to **Nike** (goddess of victory), dedicated at the Sanctuary of the Great Gods (Cabeiri) on Samothrace. The Nike stood atop a stone ship's prow, announcing the dedication with her now-lost trumpet or gesture.
- **Content.** **Nike** striding forward, wings spread, wet chiton pressed against her body by sea wind; no head, no arms. Originally cupped hands or trumpet to herald a victory. A fountain once fed by water animated the scene.
- **Form.** Subtractive **Parian marble**. The body coils into a **spiral composition**; tense forward movement against the wind. **Wet drapery** clings and billows, describing the body beneath. Pure white, fine-grained marble; designed for a **left-front viewing angle** — the right side is less detailed.

**Vocabulary:** subtractive · sanctuary · votive
