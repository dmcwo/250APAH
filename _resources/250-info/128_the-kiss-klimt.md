## #128 — The Kiss

**Gustav Klimt** (Art Nouveau / Vienna Secession) · 1907–1908 C.E. · Oil and gold leaf on canvas · Österreichische Galerie Belvedere, Vienna · 5′11″ × 5′11″

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/The_Kiss_-_Gustav_Klimt_-_Google_Cultural_Institute.jpg/330px-The_Kiss_-_Gustav_Klimt_-_Google_Cultural_Institute.jpg" width="240" alt="The Kiss by Klimt">

### Summary

The signature painting of Klimt's **Golden Phase** and of Viennese **Art Nouveau**.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in Vienna during Klimt's 1898–1908 **Golden Phase**, heavily influenced by the **Byzantine mosaics** he had seen in Ravenna and by the Vienna Secession / Arts and Crafts push against academic art. The sensual theme continues Klimt's earlier *Beethoven Frieze* and *Stoclet Frieze*; unlike his "pornographic" Vienna Ceiling paintings, *The Kiss* was embraced by critics.
- **Function.** A universal, sensual image of love made in a period of urban modernization and anxiety — meant to freeze an ideal, all-consuming embrace.
- **Content.** A kneeling couple lock in a kiss on the edge of a flowering meadow. The man wears a gold robe patterned with **black-and-white rectangles** and a crown of vines; the woman wears a tight dress with floral **circles and ovals**, her hair scattered with flowers that read as a halo. The woman is the emotional protagonist — she receives the kiss actively, not as passive object.
- **Form.** Oil on canvas with layered **gold and silver leaf** that produces a flat, non-spatial ground. Linear/rectangular male ornament set against curvilinear/floral female ornament; heads pushed toward the top of the canvas in the manner of Japanese woodblock composition.

**Vocabulary:** Art Nouveau · Arts and Crafts · Vienna Secession · gold leaf
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 2.3, 3.2
