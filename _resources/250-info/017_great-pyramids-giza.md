## #17 — Great Pyramids (Menkaura, Khafre, Khufu) and Great Sphinx

**Egyptian, Old Kingdom, Fourth Dynasty** · c. 2550–2490 B.C.E. · Cut limestone, granite · Giza, Egypt · Khufu: 455 ft tall; Khafre: 448 ft; Menkaura: 213 ft; Sphinx: 66 × 63 × 238 ft

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Pyramids_of_the_Giza_Necropolis.jpg/330px-Pyramids_of_the_Giza_Necropolis.jpg" width="240" alt="Great Pyramids of Giza">

### Summary

Three monumental **royal tombs** for the pharaohs **Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaura**, guarded by the **Great Sphinx** — emblems of Old Kingdom power, geometry, and funerary belief.

- **Historical Context.** Built during the Old Kingdom (Fourth Dynasty), when a highly centralized theocratic state headed by a god-king enabled projects at this scale. Located beside the **Nile**, whose predictable flooding underwrote Egyptian agriculture; the dominant solar deity was **Ra**. The pyramids decrease in size in each generation, tracking the kingdom's declining resources.
- **Function.** **Funerary monuments** to protect and provision the pharaoh on his journey to the **afterlife**, and to project the state's power. Interior chambers hold the sarcophagus and grave goods; **false doors and shafts** deterred tomb robbers. The **Great Sphinx** (likely Khafre's face on a lion body) guarded Khafre's causeway.
- **Content.** Three nearly perfect **pyramidal** masses, corners aligned to the **cardinal directions**; each sits within a complex that once included a mortuary temple and causeway. The **Sphinx**, carved **in situ** from the bedrock, was Egypt's first colossal sculpture.
- **Form.** Stacked limestone cores with **Tura limestone casing** and red granite details. Massive, geometrically pure silhouettes; smooth, originally polished surfaces; hieroglyphic-lined interior chambers.

**Vocabulary:** in situ · hieratic (hierarchical) scale · necropolis · ka · hieroglyphics
