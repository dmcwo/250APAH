## #171 — Ndop (portrait figure) of King Mishe miShyaang maMbul

**Kuba peoples** (Democratic Republic of the Congo) · c. 1760–1780 C.E. · Wood (*crossopterix febrifuga*), camwood powder · Brooklyn Museum · 19½ × 7⅝ × 8⅝ in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Brooklyn_Museum_61.33_Ndop_Portrait_of_King_Mishe_miShyaang_maMbul_%285%29.jpg/250px-Brooklyn_Museum_61.33_Ndop_Portrait_of_King_Mishe_miShyaang_maMbul_%285%29.jpg" width="240" alt="Ndop of King Mishe miShyaang maMbul">

### Summary

A **Kuba royal portrait figure** that serves as the king's spiritual double.

- **Historical Context.** Made by the **Kuba peoples** in present-day DRC (Kasai region). Tradition holds that each Kuba **nyim** (ruler) commissioned an *ndop* — "statue" — upon his investiture. Only one could be made per king, and he had to be physically present for the carving. If the king were injured in battle, a matching mark was said to appear on the statue. If the ndop decayed, it could be recarved.
- **Function.** A **spiritual double** of the king, believed to house his *double* or counterpart-soul. Kept in the royal quarters; placed near women of the royal harem during childbirth to ensure safe delivery; served as a surrogate for the king in his absence. After his death it was stored away and exhibited only on rare occasions.
- **Content.** The king sits **cross-legged** on a rectangular platform with geometric bands. His right hand rests on his knee; his left holds the *ikul*, a ceremonial peace knife. Headdress consists of a decorated board atop a cylindrical ring. A cylindrical drum on a small pedestal sits in front of him — the personal **ibol** (emblem) that distinguishes this king from other ndop. Closed eyes; calm, formal expression. Not a literal likeness but an idealized composite of royal characteristics.
- **Form.** Carved single block of hardwood, anointed with palm oil and red **camwood powder**. **One-to-three proportion** — the head is one-third of the entire figure, reflecting the Kuba belief that the head is the seat of intelligence.

**Vocabulary:** ndop · nyim · ikul · ibol
