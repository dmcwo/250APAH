## #112 — Palace of Westminster (Houses of Parliament)

**Charles Barry and Augustus W. N. Pugin (architects)** · late middle ages, rebuilt 1840–1870 C.E. · Limestone masonry and glass · London, England (along the Thames) · 3.24 hectares (8 acres)

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Houses_of_Parliament_in_2022_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Houses_of_Parliament_in_2022_%28cropped%29.jpg" width="240" alt="Palace of Westminster">

### Summary

A **Gothic Revival** seat of British parliamentary democracy, rebuilt on a site used for government since the 13th century.

- **Historical Context.** The first royal palace on the site (Thorney Island) was built in the late 11th century and served as a royal residence until a 1512 fire. The palace has housed **Parliament** since the 13th century and was also home to the Royal Courts of Justice. On **October 16, 1834**, most of the buildings burned down (though Westminster Hall, the Cloisters of St Stephen's, and the Jewel Tower survived). A public competition in 1836 required **Gothic or Elizabethan** style — both representing conservative values over the era's popular Neoclassicism. **Charles Barry** (experienced in classical architecture) won with **Pugin** (a Gothic expert) assisting. Mostly completed by 1870.
- **Function.** Meeting place of the **House of Commons and House of Lords**. The Palace — and especially the Elizabethan tower (Big Ben) — is a symbol of parliamentary democracy.
- **Content.** Over 1,100 rooms, 100 staircases, and 3 miles of passageways across four floors. Three main towers: **Victoria Tower** (tallest, above the Sovereign's entrance), the octagonal Central Tower (for ventilation), and **Elizabeth Tower** (four-faced clock bell tower). Three separate entrances for the Monarch, Parliament members, and the public. Extended into the Thames by reclaiming ~8 acres of land.
- **Form.** Built in the **Perpendicular Gothic** style, popular in the 15th century and during the 19th-century Gothic Revival. Sand-colored **limestone from Anston quarry** was cheap and ideal for elaborate carvings. Numerous windows and towers give the structure a light, airy appearance.

**Vocabulary:** tower · pointed arch · ribbed vault · arcade
