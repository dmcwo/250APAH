## #84 — Mosque of Selim II

**Sinan (architect)** · 1568–1575 C.E. · Brick and stone · Edirne, Turkey · 190 × 130 m (complex)

### Summary

Mimar **Sinan's** self-proclaimed masterpiece — the structural climax of **Ottoman imperial mosque** design.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by Sultan **Selim II**, son of Suleiman the Magnificent, for **Edirne**, the Ottomans' former Balkan capital and the first major city European travelers encountered. Finished the year after Selim's death, 1575. Sinan set out to surpass the dome of the Byzantine **Hagia Sophia** — and claimed to have done so.
- **Function.** Congregational mosque with a full *külliye* complex: two **madrasas**, library, hospital, and shops (the shops and a Qur'an school were added under Murad III). UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2011.
- **Content.** The layout aligns **square → octagon → circle** (muezzin's platform / eight piers / dome) — a Muslim meditation on earthly vs. celestial geometry. **Iznik tiles** decorate the interior with saz leaves, Chinese-cloud motifs, and Hadith inscriptions emphasizing Islamic identity after the 1571 naval defeat at Lepanto.
- **Form.** A vast central dome (~31.25 m diameter) rests on **eight piers** and eight squinches decorated with **muqarnas**. Four slender **minarets** at the corners of the prayer hall frame the dome; 18 subsidiary domes surround it but are visually dominated by the central dome.

**Vocabulary:** minaret · madrasa · squinch · muqarna · Iznik tile
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.1, 3.2, 3.5
