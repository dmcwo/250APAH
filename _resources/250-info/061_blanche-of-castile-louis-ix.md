## #61 — Dedication Page with Blanche of Castile and King Louis IX of France, Scenes from the Apocalypse from Bibles moralisées

**Gothic Europe** · c. 1225–1245 C.E. · Illuminated manuscript (ink, tempera, and gold leaf on vellum) · Morgan Library, New York · 14¾ × 10¼ in

### Summary

A **Moralized Bible** (Bible moralisée) dedication page depicting the French **queen regent Blanche of Castile** beside her son **King Louis IX** on gold-backed thrones, with a cleric instructing a scribe below — a luxury personal Bible for a Christian king.

- **Historical Context.** When the French king died in 1226, his widow **Blanche of Castile** ruled as regent until her son **Louis IX** came of age. She defeated rebellious barons and handed her son a peaceful kingdom. Made c. 1225–1245 C.E. (likely taking eight years), the book was a lavish royal commission.
- **Function.** **Personal devotional book** for Louis IX — a volume of scriptural morals and lessons to guide his reign. The Apocalypse scenes offered meditative material on John's Revelation, reinforcing Christian kingship.
- **Content.** Dedication page: Blanche and Louis IX enthroned under trilobed arches on a gold background — a visual parallel to **Virgin and Christ enthroned in heaven**, sanctioning the king's divine authority. Below, a cleric instructs a scribe in the production of the book. The Apocalypse scenes narrate John's Vision in eight medallions with Latin text.
- **Form.** **Ink, tempera, and gold leaf on vellum**. Gold backgrounds emphasize royalty; **royal blue** connects the page to French monarchy. Gothic proportions — small heads, attenuated figures; little perspective. Arches overhead evoke cathedral architecture.

**Vocabulary:** Moralized Bible · Apocalypse · Andachtsbild
