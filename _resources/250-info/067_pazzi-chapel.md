## #67 — Pazzi Chapel, Basilica di Santa Croce

**Filippo Brunelleschi (architect)** · c. 1429–1461 C.E. · Masonry · Florence, Italy · 36 × 60 ft

### Summary

A small Early Renaissance **chapter house** attached to **Santa Croce** — **Brunelleschi's** geometric essay in white stucco and **pietra serena**, a calm rebuttal to Gothic ornament.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by the **Pazzi family** (rival of the **Medici**) as a chapter house for the Santa Croce friars, with a family **tomb** below. Brunelleschi planned it but did not live to see it completed; construction continued through the 1460s. Part of the same early-Renaissance moment as Brunelleschi's dome on Florence Cathedral.
- **Function.** A **chapter house** (meeting place for monks) and Pazzi family burial chapel.
- **Content.** Entrance portico with barrel-vaulted ceiling; interior square centered under a small ribbed dome on pendentives; **glazed terracotta tondi** by **Luca della Robbia** depicting the Apostles in the blue-and-white vault and the Evangelists in the pendentives. **Pazzi coat of arms** and heraldic elements decorate the interior.
- **Form.** **Pietra serena** (greenish-grey stone) pilasters and architraves articulate white stucco walls — a linear grammar of **geometry**, symmetry, and clear proportion. The plan (short rectangle subdividing into three bays with a central domed square) echoes ancient central-plan architecture. Intended harmony over ornament.

**Vocabulary:** cloister · barrel vault · portico · pendentives
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.1
