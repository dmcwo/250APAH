## #58 — Church of Sainte-Foy

**Romanesque Europe** · Church c. 1050–1130 C.E.; Reliquary of Saint Foy 9th century C.E. with later additions · Stone (architecture); stone and paint (tympanum); gold, silver, gemstones, and enamel over wood (reliquary) · Conques, France · Church ~1550 m; reliquary 33.5 in

### Summary

A **Romanesque pilgrimage church** at Conques on the route to **Santiago de Compostela** — its **Last Judgment tympanum** warns pilgrims of damnation, and its reliquary of the child-martyr **Sainte-Foy** is encrusted with centuries of accumulated jewels.

- **Historical Context.** **Saint Foy** was a 12-year-old Christian girl martyred in 2nd-century Gaul for refusing to abjure her faith. The abbey at Conques was a stop on the **pilgrimage route to Santiago de Compostela**; pilgrim devotion funded the church. The bejeweled reliquary drew debate — **Bernard of Angers** worried that it blurred the line between veneration and idolatry.
- **Function.** A **pilgrimage church** and monastic abbey. Its cruciform plan with wide aisles and an ambulatory allowed pilgrim circulation around the relics without disturbing monastic worship.
- **Content.** The **west tympanum** depicts the **Last Judgment**: enthroned Christ points one hand to the saved (heaven, ordered) and the other to the damned (hell, chaotic), with the weighing of souls below. The reliquary statue of Sainte-Foy holds martyrdom attributes and is covered in **jewels and ancient cameos** donated by pilgrims over centuries.
- **Form.** **Romanesque** barrel-vaulted nave with arcades and stone columns; **cruciform plan** (symbol of Christ's cross). The tympanum is painted stone **relief**. The reliquary is carved wood sheathed in gold, silver, and gems — a reuse and aggregation across eras.

**Vocabulary:** pilgrim · Romanesque · abbey · tympanum · reliquary
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.1, 2.2, 2.3
