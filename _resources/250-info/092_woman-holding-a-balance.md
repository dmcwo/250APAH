## #92 — Woman Holding a Balance

**Johannes Vermeer** (Dutch Baroque) · c. 1664 C.E. · Oil on canvas · National Gallery of Art, Washington D.C. · 39.7 × 35.5 cm

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Johannes_Vermeer_-_Woman_Holding_a_Balance_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/330px-Johannes_Vermeer_-_Woman_Holding_a_Balance_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" width="240" alt="Woman Holding a Balance">

### Summary

A Dutch Baroque meditation on moral balance, juxtaposing earthly wealth with the Last Judgment.

- **Historical Context.** Vermeer (b. Delft, 1632 – d. 1675) spent his life in Delft; the seated woman is likely his wife Catharina. The painting-within-a-painting of the **Last Judgment** hangs directly behind her, connecting her "mundane act" to the Weighing of Souls. Her blue robe and downcast gaze recall images of the **Virgin Mary**; her costume reflects mid-1660s Dutch fashion.
- **Function.** Painted for a private home. An allegory urging temperance — weighing unborn souls and material possessions against the religious themes of the painting above.
- **Content.** A young woman, possibly pregnant, holds an **empty** balance before a table of jewelry boxes overflowing with pearls and gold. A mirror and draped window are at left; the *Last Judgment* painting fills the wall behind her, with Christ's figure directly above her head.
- **Form.** Vermeer likely used a **camera obscura**; orthogonals converge precisely at her fingertip, the picture's vanishing point. Trademark blue/grey/yellow palette; pearls rendered in two layers (gray glaze + thick specular highlight); light seeps in through a gap in the curtains.

**Vocabulary:** Intimate interior scene · Last Judgment · painting within a painting · balance · spiritual vs. materialistic · camera obscura · opulent furnishings · pearls · chiaroscuro
