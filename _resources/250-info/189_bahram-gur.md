## #189 — Bahram Gur Fights the Karg, folio from the Great Il-Khanid *Shahnama*

**Persian, Ilkhanate** · 1330–1340 C.E. · Ink and opaque watercolor on paper · Harvard Art Museums · 41.5 × 30 cm

### Summary

A folio of Persian heroic mythology from the **Great Il-Khanid Shahnama**, made for a Mongol-ruled court.

- **Historical Context.** Produced in **Tabriz**, capital of the Muslim-Mongol **Ilkhanate** (the Iranian successor state to the Mongol Empire). The Il-Khanids were Persian-Mongol rulers patronizing Persian cultural production as political legitimation after conversion to Islam. This particular manuscript is one of the most lavish Persian manuscripts ever made. Paper, rather than vellum, shows Islamic technological sophistication.
- **Function.** Illuminated pages from the **Shahnama** (Book of Kings) — the Persian national epic by Ferdowsi, tracing Persian kings from pre-history to Alexander. Educated young nobles in the glory and proper behavior of their Persian heritage; reinforced the Ilkhanid dynasty's claim to Persian legitimacy.
- **Content.** The Persian hero **Bahram Gur** has just slain the **karg**, a mythic wolf-rhinoceros monster. He stands in heroic pose with his stallion rearing on its hind legs and his mace slung over his shoulder, looking off into the sunset. A golden halo surrounds him. Bahram's features appear **Mongol**, signaling the cultural fusion of the Ilkhanate.
- **Form.** **Ink and opaque watercolor on paper**; composition dominated by the single figure and rearing horse; limited depth but strong compositional energy.
