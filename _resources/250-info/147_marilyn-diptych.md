## #147 — Marilyn Diptych

**Andy Warhol** (Pop Art) · 1962 C.E. · Oil, acrylic, and silkscreen enamel on canvas · Tate Modern, London · 81 × 114 in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/8/87/Marilyndiptych.jpg" width="240" alt="Marilyn Diptych">

### Summary

Warhol's Pop-art memorial for **Marilyn Monroe**, turning celebrity into a printed icon.

- **Historical Context.** Created in August 1962 immediately after Marilyn Monroe's death; first shown at the Stable Gallery in New York. Warhol was steeped in American pop culture and was a **Ruthenian Catholic** — the diptych format echoes religious altarpieces. Marilyn was the most famous **sex symbol** of the 1950s.
- **Function.** A commemoration of Monroe and a meditation on fame, mass-reproduction, life, and death. The contrast between color and greyscale halves stages Monroe's vitality on the left and her fade into death on the right.
- **Content.** The same 1953 publicity photograph for the film *Niagara*, repeated 25 times in color (left) and 25 times in greyscale (right) — the right side gradually degrading to near-nothing.
- **Form.** **Silkscreen** on canvas — mesh and stencils transfer the photograph onto the surface, making the painting a mechanical reproduction like a newspaper page. Faces differ in ink density; the flat, commercial palette (yellow hair, pink face, red lips) intensifies the celebrity-image effect.

**Vocabulary:** iconic · abstract expressionism · repetition · pop culture · emotionally flat
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.3, 3.1
