## #212 — Chairman Mao en Route to Anyuan

**Liu Chunhua (China)** · 1967 C.E. · Color lithograph based on an oil painting · Guangzhou, China (private collection) · 29.5 × 21.6 in

### Summary

The most widely reproduced image of the **Cultural Revolution**, depicting a youthful Mao striding into history with messianic purpose.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in 1967 by **Liu Chunhua**, a student at the Central Academy of Arts and Crafts and a member of the **Red Guard**, for an exhibition commemorating the 1922 **Anyuan Miners' Strike**. The strike was the Communist Party's first major labor action; later Maoist historiography exaggerated Mao's role in it at the expense of Liu Shaoqi. At the height of the Cultural Revolution, the painting was reproduced an estimated **900 million times** in posters, pins, and stamps.
- **Function.** **Propaganda** — builds the Mao personality cult and legitimizes the Cultural Revolution by casting Mao's early career as heroic, predestined struggle.
- **Content.** Mao, depicted as a 28-year-old, strides down a mountain path in a traditional blue **cheongsam** gown (rather than a Western suit). His left fist is clenched in determination; his right hand holds a worn paper umbrella, symbol of humble persistence. Wind ruffles his hair and robes; dark storm clouds rise behind him.
- **Form.** Mao-era **Socialist Realism** with Chinese inflections. Atmospheric perspective places Mao against luminous light that haloes him as a sun-like figure; cool palette suggests pre-revolutionary dawn. Western oil-painting techniques adapted to Chinese political iconography.

**Vocabulary:** atmospheric perspective · propaganda · socialist realism · color lithograph
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 2.1, 3.2
