## #107 — La Grande Odalisque

**Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres** · 1814 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Louvre, Paris · 35″ × 64″

### Summary

A **Neoclassical-to-Romantic** hybrid — a deliberately anatomically distorted nude steeped in **Orientalist** fantasy.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by **Caroline Murat**, Napoleon's sister and Queen of Naples. Ingres was trained by **Jacques-Louis David** and considered himself a Neoclassicist, but his elongated figures and fascination with "Oriental" subjects anticipate **Romanticism**. The painting scandalized the 1819 Salon; critics complained the odalisque had several extra vertebrae.
- **Function.** Private **erotic commission** for a royal patron. The subject capitalized on European fascination with Ottoman harem culture while providing cover for an otherwise transgressive nude.
- **Content.** A **nude concubine** reclines on a bed of satin sheets, her back to the viewer, head turned to gaze calmly over her shoulder. She holds a **peacock-feather fan**; a turban, pearl jewelry, and a **hookah** anchor her in a fantasy harem setting.
- **Form.** Smooth, porcelain flesh; cool jewel-tone silks; **dark tenebrist background**. Ingres deliberately lengthens the spine and limbs to heighten the **serpentine line** and eroticism — privileging beauty over anatomy. Deep shadow half-veils her face.

**Vocabulary:** Romanticism · Neoclassicism · oil painting · Oriental · tenebrism · portraiture
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3, 2.1
