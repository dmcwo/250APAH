## #172 — Power figure (Nkisi n'kondi)

**Kongo peoples, Yombe group** (Democratic Republic of the Congo) · c. late 19th century C.E. · Wood and metal · 36 × 18½ × 14¼ in.

### Summary

A **Kongo power figure** bristling with nails — each one a sworn oath or broken promise.

- **Historical Context.** Made by the **Yombe** group of the Kongo peoples. The Yombe migrated south from present-day Gabon before the 15th century and came into extensive contact with the **Portuguese** beginning in the 16th century. **Ngoma Bunzi** was the supreme god, approached only through intermediary earth spirits (*Nzambi a Tsi*) and river spirits (*Simbi*).
- **Function.** A tool of justice and protection. Once the sculpture was carved, a ritual specialist, the **nganga**, activated it by packing medicinal substances (**bilongo**) into cavities in the head and stomach and sealing them with mirrors — glass through which spirits of the dead could see enemies. Every time someone needed to seal an oath, cure an illness, settle a dispute, or curse an enemy, a nail or blade was driven in — "waking" the spirit inside.
- **Content.** Carved in **anthropomorphic** standing form with wide eyes, imposing stance, belly-button cavity sealed with a **cowrie shell**. The outer surface is covered with nails, pegs, blades, cloth strips, rope, beads — added over a long period of use.
- **Form.** Wood core carved by the sculptor; the accumulation of nails, medicine bundles (*bilongo*), mirrors, and attached objects transforms the piece over time through ritual use rather than a single artistic gesture.

**Vocabulary:** nkisi · nganga · bilongo · mpemba
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4, 3.1, 3.2
