## #110 — Still Life in Studio

**Louis-Jacques-Mandé Daguerre** · 1837 C.E. · Daguerreotype · French Photographic Society, Paris · ~6.5 × 8.5 in

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Daguerreotype_Daguerre_Atelier_1837.jpg" width="240" alt="Still Life in Studio">

### Summary

Daguerre's earliest surviving daguerreotype — a test image that announced photography's potential as both science and art form.

- **Historical Context.** Created in the infancy of photography, ten years after Niépce's **View from the Window at Le Gras** (c. 1827), which required an 8-hour exposure. Acquainted with Niépce, Daguerre sought to solve the issues of long exposures and light sensitivity. Using a **copper plate with silver coating** and halogens like iodine and bromine, Daguerre captured crisp images in **15–30 minutes**, later preserved with mercury and a sodium compound. The Daguerreian Process was publicly released in **1839**; exposures dropped below a minute by 1842.
- **Function.** As Daguerre's earliest surviving daguerreotype, most likely a **test** of his photographic process. The diverse textures (fabric, stucco, plaster, painting, metal, wood, wicker) let him analyze his process's ability to capture tonal range. The two stucco heads substituted for real people, since portraiture was not yet possible at those exposure times. Some interpret the inclusion of sculpture and a framed painting as an argument for photography as a new **art form**.
- **Content.** An arrangement of inanimate studio objects with no living figures (though two stucco human heads appear). A test composition of textures and tones.
- **Form.** Overlapping arrangement creates negative space and perspective depth — the curtain is blocked by a wicker pot holder, placed behind the stucco heads. Intense **chiaroscuro** (approaching tenebrism): light from the window throws sharp shadows on the heads. Diagonal lines on the left panel direct attention to the stucco heads as focal point.

**Vocabulary:** Daguerreotype (Daguerreian Process) · camera obscura
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3, 2.3, 3.2, 3.5
