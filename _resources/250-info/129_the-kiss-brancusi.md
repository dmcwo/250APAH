## #129 — The Kiss

**Constantin Brâncuși** · 1907–1908 C.E. (this version 1916) · Limestone · Philadelphia Museum of Art · 11 × 10¼ × 8½ in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/The_Kiss_LCCN93511527.jpg/250px-The_Kiss_LCCN93511527.jpg" width="240" alt="The Kiss by Brancusi">

### Summary

A radical **proto-Cubist** reduction of the embrace to a single, nearly monolithic block.

- **Historical Context.** Brâncuși, a Romanian sculptor working in Paris, made the first version in 1907–1908 and returned to the subject repeatedly. This limestone version was carved in 1916 on commission from Brâncuși's New York patron **John Quinn** after the original had already been given away. It sits within the larger **proto-Cubist** turn toward extreme geometrization that was reshaping European sculpture alongside Picasso's and Braque's painting.
- **Function.** A deliberate counter-statement to the elaborate sentimental versions of *The Kiss* that preceded it (notably **Rodin**'s 1882 bronze and Klimt's 1908 painting), recasting the subject as stone simplicity.
- **Content.** Two figures locked face-to-face, carved from a single block. A vertical line separates them; a small curve marks the woman's breast; longer hair distinguishes her; the two eyes merge into one almost cyclopic form; pairs of arms wrap around the sides to form the embrace.
- **Form.** A cubic limestone mass, almost symmetrical, with only minimal incised lines for arms, hair, eyes, and mouth. Brâncuși refused any base — he said "any kind of arrangement will have the look of an amputation."

**Vocabulary:** geometrization · avant-garde · proto-Cubism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.2, 2.3, 3.2
