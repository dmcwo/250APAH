## #42 — Head of a Roman Patrician

**Republican Roman** · c. 75–50 B.C.E. · Marble · Palazzo Torlonia, Rome (from Otricoli) · Bust-sized

### Summary

A **veristic** Republican portrait of an unknown, elderly Roman **patrician** — wrinkles, sunken cheeks, and hooked nose rendered to advertise **gravitas** and accumulated service, the opposite of Greek idealization.

- **Historical Context.** Carved in late Republican Rome, when senate-level **patricians** ran the state; aristocratic lineage and political accomplishment were signaled by images of **age, not youth**. The style is **verism**, an exaggerated naturalism likely influenced by Etruscan ancestor portraits and Greek Hellenistic realism.
- **Function.** A **portrait bust** celebrating a specific patrician's career — probably displayed in his atrium (alongside wax death masks, *imagines*, of ancestors) and shown in public processions to advertise the family's political pedigree.
- **Content.** A portrait of an unnamed elderly man with a **hooked nose**, deeply **furrowed brow**, sagging jowls, and thin-lipped mouth — an image of **experience, authority, and civic virtue**. No dynamism; direct, emotionless gaze.
- **Form.** Marble bust carved in **verism** — exaggerated wrinkles and sags without adornment. Hair is minimal; attention is concentrated in the expressive topography of the face.

**Vocabulary:** verism · gravitas · (lack of) dynamism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.1, 1.4, 2.1, 3.1, 3.2
