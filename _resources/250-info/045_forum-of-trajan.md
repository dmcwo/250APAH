## #45 — Forum of Trajan

**Imperial Roman; Apollodorus of Damascus** · Forum and markets 106–112 C.E.; column completed 113 C.E. · Brick and concrete (architecture); marble (column) · Rome, Italy · 1000 × 600 ft

### Summary

The **grandest imperial forum** in Rome — a complex of plaza, basilica, libraries, multilevel markets, and a narrative **column** — commemorating Trajan's conquest of **Dacia**.

- **Historical Context.** Designed by **Apollodorus of Damascus** (architect of the Pantheon) for **Trajan** ("Optimus Princeps"), whose conquests pushed the Empire to its greatest extent. Construction required cutting away a 40-ft hill.
- **Function.** Civic and commemorative. The forum hosted public life (courts, legal proceedings, political gatherings); the Basilica Ulpia served legal and administrative needs; twin Greek and Latin **libraries** flanked the **Column of Trajan**; the multilevel **Markets of Trajan** housed 150+ shops — a progenitor of the shopping mall.
- **Content.** Sequential axial spaces: entry arch → vast colonnaded forum (with equestrian bronze of Trajan) → **Basilica Ulpia** → libraries → **Column of Trajan** rising between them, its continuous helical relief narrating the Dacian Wars. The **semicircular exedra** of Trajan's Markets wraps one side.
- **Form.** Concrete-and-brick construction faced in marble. Monumental axial plan with symmetry, colonnades, triumphal arches. The **125-ft marble column** carries a spiraling band of figural relief roughly 600 ft long — a pioneering continuous narrative.

**Vocabulary:** column · arch · portico · exedra · basilica · relief · arcade
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3, 1.4, 3.5
