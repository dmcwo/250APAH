## #100 — A Philosopher Giving a Lecture on the Orrery

**Joseph Wright of Derby** · c. 1763–1765 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Derby Museum and Art Gallery · 4′ 10″ × 6′ 8″

### Summary

The unofficial painter of the **British Enlightenment** turns a science demonstration into a secular altarpiece.

- **Historical Context.** Painted during the **Age of Enlightenment** in Derby, a Midlands hub of the Industrial Revolution where Wright was connected to the **Lunar Society** (Erasmus Darwin, Josiah Wedgwood, James Watt). As science displaced religion at the center of intellectual life, Wright translated Baroque devotional compositions into images of laboratory wonder.
- **Function.** A scene of **public scientific demonstration**. An *orrery* is a mechanical model of the solar system. The lamp at the center plays the sun; audience members — adults and children — react to the demonstration.
- **Content.** A **gray-haired philosopher** gestures over the orrery; a man takes notes; children lean in with wonder; a young man shields his eyes from the glow; a seated man listens rapt. The figures encircle the instrument like saints around a holy relic.
- **Form.** Dramatic **chiaroscuro** — hidden light source at the orrery's center throws faces in and out of shadow, straight from Caravaggio. Oil on canvas; warm amber light against deep blacks; composition echoes religious Nativities or *sacra conversazione*, secularized for the age of reason.

**Vocabulary:** Age of Enlightenment · "scientific thinking" · chiaroscuro · orrery
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.1, 2.2
