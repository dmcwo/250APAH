## #71 — Madonna and Child with Two Angels

**Fra Filippo Lippi** · c. 1465 C.E. · Tempera on wood · Uffizi, Florence · 635 × 950 mm

### Summary

A landmark of early Italian Renaissance **humanization** of sacred figures.

- **Historical Context.** Painted in Florence by the Carmelite friar Fra Filippo Lippi, a leading figure of the early Italian Renaissance influenced by **Masaccio**. By the mid-15th century artists were recognized as intellectuals, and Madonna-and-Child scenes were a standard commission; the Medici family patronized Lippi extensively.
- **Function.** A private devotional panel, likely a Medici-circle commission; the specific patron is not documented. The intimate scale suits domestic veneration rather than altar display.
- **Content.** The youthful Mary is shown in profile at prayer; the Christ Child is held forward by two smiling, child-like angels. One of Lippi's own lovers reportedly modeled for Mary. The halos are thin, almost transparent; pearls signal the Immaculate Conception; a distant seashore and city frame a symbolic "Heavenly Jerusalem."
- **Form.** A Florentine interior frames an atmospheric landscape with rocks, sea, and a distant city. Trompe-l'oeil architectural frame, naturalized anatomy, asymmetrical composition, and diminished halos emphasize the **humanity** of holy figures — a hallmark of the Italian Renaissance going back to Giotto.

**Vocabulary:** tempera · Italian Renaissance · Madonna and Child · naturalism · humanization · atmospheric perspective · trompe l'oeil · Florentine
