## #101 — The Swing

**Jean-Honoré Fragonard** · 1767 C.E. · Oil on canvas · Wallace Collection, London · 81 × 64.2 cm

### Summary

The definitive **French Rococo** painting — playful, erotic, and wealth-drenched.

- **Historical Context.** Fragonard worked during the late reign of Louis XV, when aristocratic France indulged in lighthearted, ornate **Rococo** painting in retreat from Baroque grandeur. His patrons included Madame de Pompadour and Jean Chardin; this commission came from the **Baron de Saint-Julien**, Receiver General of the French Clergy, who specified the scene's erotic conceit himself.
- **Function.** A **private erotic cabinet picture** commissioned to illustrate a love triangle: Saint-Julien, his mistress, and an oblivious cuckolded husband or bishop.
- **Content.** A young woman soars on a swing in a wooded garden, kicking her **shoe** into the air; her lover lies hidden in the bushes at left, staring up her billowing skirt and pointing at her with his outstretched hat. An older, cuckolded man at right pushes the swing, unaware. Two **putti** witness the scene; one puts a finger to its lips (silence).
- **Form.** Asymmetrical Rococo composition. Pastel pink-and-green palette, feathery brushwork, atmospheric garden setting, abundance of ornate detail (ruffles, foliage, statuary). Strong diagonal lines sweep from lower left to upper right.

**Vocabulary:** oil on canvas · Rococo · putto
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.3
