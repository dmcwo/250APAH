## #44 — Colosseum (Flavian Amphitheater)

**Imperial Roman, Flavian dynasty** · 70–80 C.E. · Stone and concrete · Rome, Italy · 615 × 510 ft; ~50,000–80,000 spectators

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Colosseo_2020.jpg/500px-Colosseo_2020.jpg" width="240" alt="Colosseum">

### Summary

The largest amphitheater ever built — begun by **Vespasian**, completed by **Titus**, named for the Flavian dynasty — funded by spoils from the **Great Jewish Revolt** and fitted with sophisticated concrete engineering.

- **Historical Context.** Built to return to the Roman people land Nero had seized for his Domus Aurea. Financed by the sack of Jerusalem (70 C.E.); constructed in part by some **100,000 Jewish prisoners**. Opened with **100 days of games** under Titus.
- **Function.** Mass entertainment: **gladiatorial combat, wild-animal hunts,** and even flooded **mock naval battles** (naumachiae). 80 entrances managed crowd flow; the **hypogeum** (below-stage) raised animals and combatants into the arena via a pulley system; the **velarium** (awning) shaded spectators above.
- **Content.** An ellipse of stacked stone arcades. Sand (*arena*) covered the floor to absorb blood; statues once filled the upper arches; bronze shields decorated the attic.
- **Form.** **Concrete** construction with **travertine limestone** facing — the engineering breakthrough that allowed free-standing mass entertainment venues. Four stories with stacked column orders: **Tuscan, Ionic, Corinthian, Composite** engaged pilasters frame rhythmic arcades. Barrel-vaulted radial corridors distribute spectators.

**Vocabulary:** amphitheatre · velarium · hypogeum
**Learning Objectives:** LO 2.1, 2.2
