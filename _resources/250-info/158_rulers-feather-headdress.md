## #158 — Ruler's feather headdress (probably of Motecuhzoma II)

**Mexica (Aztec)** · 1428–1520 C.E. · Feathers (quetzal and cotinga) and gold · Museum of Ethnology, Vienna · 46 × 69 in.

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/dd/Feather_headdress_Moctezuma_II.JPG" width="240" alt="Ruler's feather headdress">

### Summary

The only surviving Aztec **feather headdress** and reputedly a gift from **Motecuhzoma II** to Cortés.

- **Historical Context.** Traditionally identified as **Motecuhzoma II's** *quetzalapanecayotl*, probably given to **Hernán Cortés** during the conquest and shipped to **Charles V**, Holy Roman Emperor. Now in Vienna's Weltmuseum. A replica using traditional techniques sits in the National Museum of Anthropology in Mexico City.
- **Function.** A regalia / display object signaling royal and divine authority; possibly worn on the back of a shield-device rather than atop the head. As a diplomatic gift it performed Aztec political importance for a foreign conqueror.
- **Content.** 400 long green feathers from the tail of the **resplendent quetzal** (each male bird produces only two per year, making the feathers extraordinarily rare and sacred) plus blue **cotinga** feathers, arranged in a semicircular fan with gold disks and bead-work at the base. The number 400 symbolizes eternity in Mexica numerology.
- **Form.** Semicircular fan radiating from a gold-decorated band. The neon-green quetzal feathers dominate; gold accents form a halo-like disc above the wearer. Symmetrical and balanced, meant to extend and magnify the wearer's head.

**Vocabulary:** Aztec · Moctezuma II · Cortés · Charles V · quetzal bird · Tenochtitlan
