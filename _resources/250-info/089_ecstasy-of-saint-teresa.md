## #89 — Ecstasy of Saint Teresa

**Gian Lorenzo Bernini** · c. 1647–1652 C.E. · Marble (sculpture); stucco and gilt bronze (chapel) · Cornaro Chapel, Santa Maria della Vittoria, Rome · life size

### Summary

The **archetypal Italian Baroque *Gesamtkunstwerk*** — sculpture, architecture, painting, and light staged as theater.

- **Historical Context.** Commissioned by Cardinal **Federico Cornaro** for his family chapel during the **Counter-Reformation**. The subject is **St. Teresa of Ávila** (1515–1582), co-founder of the Discalced Carmelites, recently canonized (1622); her memoir described an angel repeatedly piercing her heart with a golden arrow, leaving her in mystical "sweet pain."
- **Function.** An altar shrine meant to induce piety in viewers by dramatizing a saint's private vision. The Cornaro donors appear in relief in the "theater boxes" flanking the stage.
- **Content.** St. Teresa swoons on a cloud, eyes closed, mouth open, foot dangling; a smiling **angel** prepares to plunge his golden arrow into her heart. Above, a **hidden window** pours natural light through **gilt-bronze rays**, concentrating divine grace on the figures.
- **Form.** Virtuoso **marble** rendered as weightless cloud and fluttering drapery. Dynamic diagonal composition, extreme emotion, airy textures contrasted against architectural niche. Combines sculpture, architecture, stucco, fresco, and concealed lighting into a unified multi-media illusion.

**Vocabulary:** gesamtkunstwerk · Baroque · bridal mysticism
**Learning Objectives:** LO 1.2, 1.3, 3.2
